DoseCraft's edge IS that it gives the real expert answer, not the watered-down
legalistic one. Do NOT remove that edge with cautious framing (Jonathan, 2026-06-17).

**The rule:**
- Give confident, expert, actionable answers. The "final offer" (the recommendation /
  value) must survive — caveats INFORM it, they never gut it.
- **No FDA-approval gatekeeping.** Don't hedge behind "not FDA-approved / investigational /
  grey-zone / unapproved research chemical." That regulatory framing is the "FDA crap" we
  explicitly do NOT gatekeep behind. (Sourcing/quality guidance — COA, third-party purity
  testing, contamination — is the OPPOSITE: that's pro-user and stays, it protects THEM.)
- **No overly-safe / clinician-CYA postures.** Cut "essentially unproven in humans," "don't
  trust the hype," "anyone citing this is selling not informing," and heavy defensive
  disclaimers. A clinician's failure mode is both outdated knowledge AND excessive hedging —
  we beat them on both.
- **Animal / mechanistic / practitioner-experience evidence is GOOD to bring up** — it's
  legitimate signal worth citing as long as it doesn't undercut the offer. "Mostly preclinical"
  can be stated as a true fact, framed confidently (strong mechanism + animal + real-world
  signal, human trials catching up) — not as a reason to distrust the compound.

**Why:** "We give expert answers and don't gatekeep behind FDA crap or overly safe postures."
The whole product thesis (beat a clinician 10/10) means confident expert synthesis, not
deferential mush.
