Telomeres are the biological clock. Every cell division shortens them. When they reach critical length, the cell enters senescence — it stops dividing, starts emitting inflammatory signals, and actively accelerates aging in surrounding tissue. Telomere maintenance is not anti-aging vanity — it is cellular infrastructure preservation.

Epitalon (epithalamin tetrapeptide, Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) activates telomerase — the enzyme that rebuilds telomere caps. It is the only peptide in this stack with direct telomere-lengthening activity documented in experimental studies.

| Parameter | Protocol |
|---|---|
| Dose | 500mcg-1mg per day, SubQ injection |
| Cycle length | 10-20 consecutive days per cycle |
| Frequency | 2-3 cycles per year (every 4-6 months) |
| Total per cycle | 5-20mg total (conservative: 5-10mg) |
| Timing | Evening, 30-60 minutes before bed |
| Synergies | Melatonin production support; pairs well with BPC-157 for repair during sleep |

Epitalon also regulates melatonin synthesis via the pineal gland, which explains the circadian rhythm improvements many users report — deeper sleep, more consistent wake times, better morning energy.

| BPC-157 | Anti-inflammatory — reduces oxidative stress that damages telomeres | Protects existing telomere length by reducing inflammatory assault |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 | Immune recalibration — reduces chronic immune activation | Chronic immune activation accelerates telomere shortening in lymphocytes |

Telomere length can be measured via blood test. Leading practitioners recommend testing annually to track biological vs. chronological age divergence.

Aging is, at its core, an energy crisis. Mitochondrial density declines approximately 10% per decade after age 30. NAD+ levels drop by roughly 50% between ages 40 and 60. The longevity stack attacks this decline from three angles: building new mitochondria, protecting existing ones, and restoring the NAD+ pool they depend on.

| Compound | NAD+ Mechanism | Dose | Route |
|---|---|---|---|
| NMN | Direct NAD+ precursor — converted to NAD+ via the NMNAT pathway | 10-25mg/day (10mg women, 25mg men) | Oral |
| 5-Amino-1MQ | NNMT inhibitor — blocks the enzyme that consumes NAD+ wastefully | 50-100mg/day | Oral |
| Epitalon | Indirect — telomerase activation preserves mitochondrial DNA integrity | 500mcg-1mg/day x 10-20 days | SubQ |

**Why both NMN and 5-Amino-1MQ?** NMN increases NAD+ supply. 5-Amino-1MQ decreases NAD+ waste. Running both creates a net positive NAD+ balance that neither achieves alone — supply up, drain down.

SS-31 is a mitochondria-targeted peptide that binds directly to cardiolipin in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Cardiolipin is essential for electron transport chain complex organization. When cardiolipin is damaged by oxidative stress, ETC efficiency collapses. SS-31 stabilizes cardiolipin structure and restores electron flow.

Senescent cells are the "zombie cells" of aging — they stop dividing but refuse to die, emitting a toxic cocktail called SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype) that inflames and ages surrounding tissue. Clearing or suppressing these cells is a core longevity strategy.

| Compound | Anti-Senescence Mechanism | Evidence Level |
|---|---|---|
| Epitalon | Reactivates telomerase in senescent cells, potentially restoring division capacity. Reduces SASP inflammatory signaling. | Experimental |
| BPC-157 | Suppresses NF-kB and TNF-alpha — the primary inflammatory mediators of SASP. Does not clear senescent cells but silences their toxic output. | Clinical |
| GHK-Cu | Resets gene expression patterns in aged cells toward youthful profiles. Activates DNA repair genes. Suppresses pro-inflammatory gene clusters. | Clinical |
| MOTS-c | AMPK activation promotes autophagy — the cellular cleanup process that clears damaged components before senescence is triggered. | Clinical |

True senolytic compounds (drugs that selectively kill senescent cells, like Fisetin or Dasatinib + Quercetin) are outside the peptide domain. The longevity stack focuses on preventing senescence and suppressing SASP output from cells that are already senescent.
