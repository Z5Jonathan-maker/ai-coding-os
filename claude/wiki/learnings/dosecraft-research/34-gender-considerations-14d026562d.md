# 34-gender-considerations

Biology differs between sexes. Dosing, hormonal interactions, and safety profiles demand gender-aware protocols.

| Compound | Women | Men | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| NMN | 10mg/day | 25mg/day | Body mass differential; hormonal metabolism differences |
| MOTS-c | 5mg/week total | 5-10mg/week total | Lower lean mass = lower metabolic demand |
| BPC-157 | 250-500mcg/day | 500mcg-1mg/day | Weight-based adjustment; mechanism identical across genders |
| RAD-140 | 5-10mg/day | 10-15mg/day | SARM sensitivity higher in women; start lower |
| Ipamorelin | 100mcg 2x/day | 200mcg 2x/day | GH response may be stronger in women at lower doses |
| Epitalon | Same: 500mcg-1mg/day for 10-20 days (5-10mg total cycle) | No gender-specific adjustment needed | |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 | Same: 1mg/day | Immune modulation dose is gender-neutral | |
| KPV | Same dosing protocol | Anti-inflammatory mechanism is gender-neutral |

Expert Dosing guidance derived from practitioner protocols — clinical trials for gender-specific peptide dosing are limited.

For premenopausal women who want to optimize peptide timing around their hormonal cycle. This is optional — most compounds work regardless of cycle phase — but strategic timing maximizes response.

| Cycle Phase | Days | Hormonal State | Optimal Compounds | Avoid/Reduce |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Menstrual | 1-5 | Low estrogen, low progesterone | BPC-157, KPV (anti-inflammatory support) | Heavy exercise + GH secretagogues |
| Follicular | 6-13 | Rising estrogen, low progesterone | GH secretagogues (peak response), MOTS-c, RAD-140 | No restrictions |
| Ovulatory | 14-16 | Peak estrogen, LH surge | Continue all; highest anabolic potential | Monitor for water retention |
| Luteal | 17-28 | Rising progesterone, declining estrogen | BPC-157, Epitalon, DSIP (sleep support) | Reduce GH secretagogue dose if using progesterone |

**ALL peptides and compounds covered in this guide are contraindicated during pregnancy and breastfeeding.** No safety data exists for fetal or neonatal exposure. If you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or nursing — stop all peptide use immediately and consult your OB-GYN.

| Compound | Effect on Fertility | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| BPC-157 | No known negative effects | Discontinue when actively trying to conceive (precautionary) |
| GH Secretagogues | May affect ovulation timing | Discontinue 1 month before planned conception |
| RAD-140 | Potential hormonal disruption | Discontinue 3+ months before planned conception (both M and F) |
| Retatrutide | GLP-1 agonists may affect implantation | Discontinue 2 months before planned conception |
| Methylene Blue | Potential teratogen | Discontinue immediately if pregnancy suspected |
| Kisspeptin | May support fertility | Being studied for fertility applications — use under medical supervision only |

Men on testosterone replacement therapy (TRT) face specific challenges: testicular atrophy, fertility suppression, and estrogen conversion. These peptide protocols work alongside TRT to mitigate side effects and optimize outcomes.

| Compound | Dose | Purpose with TRT | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gonadorelin | 100mcg SubQ 2x/week | Maintains testicular size and endogenous LH signaling while on exogenous testosterone. Prevents testicular atrophy. | Inject on non-TRT days (e.g., Mon/Thu if TRT is Tue/Fri) |
| Enclomiphene | 12.5-25mg/day oral | Selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM) that stimulates LH/FSH without anti-estrogenic side effects of clomiphene. Used for fertility preservation on TRT or as TRT alternative. | Morning with food. Can be used as monotherapy or adjunct. |
| HCG | 250-500 IU SubQ 2-3x/week | Gold standard for testicular maintenance on TRT. Mimics LH, preserves intratesticular testosterone and fertility. Maintains pregnenolone and DHEA production. | Inject on TRT injection days or EOD protocol |
| Kisspeptin-10 | 50-100mcg SubQ daily | Upstream GnRH stimulator. Experimental use for maintaining HPG axis activity during TRT. More physiological than HCG. | Morning, fasted |

Expert Gonadorelin + low-dose HCG is the most common companion stack. Add enclomiphene only if fertility preservation is a priority.

The menopausal transition brings declining estrogen, progesterone, and GH. Peptides cannot replace hormone replacement therapy (HRT), but they address the downstream metabolic, inflammatory, and regenerative consequences of hormonal decline.

| Menopause Symptom | Recommended Compounds | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Bone density loss | CJC-1295/Ipamorelin + BPC-157 | GH stimulates osteoblast activity; BPC-157 supports bone remodeling signaling |
| Accelerated skin aging | GHK-Cu (SubQ + topical) + Epitalon | GHK-Cu activates collagen synthesis genes; Epitalon supports telomere maintenance in skin cells |
| Metabolic slowdown / weight gain | MOTS-c + 5-Amino-1MQ | MOTS-c restores insulin sensitivity lost with declining estrogen; 5-Amino-1MQ boosts NAD+ and metabolic rate |
| Joint pain / inflammation | BPC-157 + TB-500 | Estrogen decline increases systemic inflammation; regenerative peptides compensate for lost protective effects |
| Sleep disruption | DSIP + Epitalon | DSIP promotes delta wave sleep; Epitalon restores declining melatonin production |
| Cognitive fog / memory | Semax + Methylene Blue (0.5mg) | BDNF upregulation; mitochondrial ATP support for neurons affected by estrogen withdrawal |
| Immune decline | Thymosin Alpha-1 | Thymic involution accelerates post-menopause; TA-1 compensates for declining immune surveillance |

Women on estrogen replacement (oral or transdermal) should separate GH secretagogue dosing from estrogen by 4+ hours. Oral estrogen increases SHBG and can blunt IGF-1 response to GH secretagogues. Transdermal estrogen has less interference. Always coordinate peptide protocols with your prescribing practitioner when on HRT.

| Age Group | Priority Compounds | Key Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| Under 30 | BPC-157, foundation supplements only | Focus on metabolic foundation. Avoid GH secretagogues unless clinically indicated. Fertility-aware — avoid RAD-140 and Retatrutide if conception possible within 6 months. |
| 30-40 | BPC-157, MOTS-c (if IR present), GHK-Cu (skin) | Periconception planning drives compound selection. Address PCOS/endometriosis with MOTS-c. Start anti-aging compounds if no pregnancy plans. |
| 40-50 (Perimenopause) | Full longevity stack at female doses, Epitalon, DSIP | GH output declining rapidly. Bone density becomes priority. Sleep disruption common — DSIP highly valuable. Start GH secretagogues if insulin is controlled. |
| 50+ (Postmenopause) | Maximum support: all applicable longevity compounds | Highest ROI decade for peptide intervention. Immune decline, sarcopenia, cognitive fog all addressable. Coordinate with HRT if prescribed. TA-1 becomes essential for immune competence. |
