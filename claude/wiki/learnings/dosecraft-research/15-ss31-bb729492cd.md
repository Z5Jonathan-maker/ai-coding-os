# 15-ss31

"The Membrane Guardian"

The only compound that targets the inner mitochondrial membrane directly — stabilizing the lipid architecture where ATP is actually produced.

SS-31 is a research compound. Clinical trials are ongoing for mitochondrial myopathies, heart failure, and age-related conditions. It is not FDA-approved for general use. All dosing information derives from published clinical trial protocols.

Every mitochondrion has an inner membrane studded with the electron transport chain — the molecular assembly line that produces ATP. The structural integrity of this membrane depends on a single, critical lipid: **cardiolipin**.

As you age, cardiolipin degrades. The membrane buckles. Electron transport chain complexes lose their orientation. Electrons leak, generating toxic reactive oxygen species (ROS) instead of ATP. SS-31 is the **only known compound that directly stabilizes cardiolipin** in the inner mitochondrial membrane.

SS-31 carries a net positive charge that drives it through both mitochondrial membranes. It concentrates **1,000-fold** within mitochondria — no other peptide achieves this specificity.

Binds directly to cardiolipin via electrostatic interactions. Restores the lipid's native conformation, re-anchoring electron transport chain complexes (especially Complexes III and IV) in their optimal positions.

By preventing electron leakage from misaligned chain complexes, SS-31 reduces mitochondrial ROS production at the source — rather than scavenging free radicals after the damage is done.

To understand why SS-31 is so significant, you need to understand cardiolipin and mitochondrial membrane potential (ΔΨm) — the two concepts that explain why mitochondria fail with age and why SS-31 is uniquely positioned to address this failure.

Cardiolipin is a unique phospholipid found almost exclusively in the inner mitochondrial membrane. It has a distinctive double-phospholipid structure (four fatty acid tails instead of the usual two), making it the structural backbone of the energy production apparatus:

The mitochondrial membrane potential is the electrical voltage difference across the inner mitochondrial membrane, created by the electron transport chain pumping protons from the matrix to the intermembrane space. This voltage (typically -150 to -180 mV in healthy mitochondria) is the **driving force behind all ATP synthesis**.

SS-31 breaks the vicious cycle by stabilizing cardiolipin before it oxidizes, preserving supercomplex architecture, and maintaining ΔΨm at levels sufficient for efficient ATP production. It addresses the root cause of mitochondrial decline rather than treating its downstream symptoms.

The EMBRACE-STEMI trial (Evaluation of Myocardial Effects of Bendavia for Reducing Reperfusion Injury in Acute Coronary Events) was a Phase 2a clinical trial evaluating SS-31 (elamipretide) in patients undergoing percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) for ST-elevation myocardial infarction (heart attack).

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Design | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, dose-ranging study |
| Population | 297 patients with first-time STEMI undergoing PCI within 4 hours |
| Administration | IV infusion of SS-31 (0.005, 0.05, or 0.25 mg/kg/hr) for 1 hour pre-PCI, continued for 4 hours post-PCI |
| Primary Endpoint | Infarct size measured by cardiac MRI at 72 hours and Day 30 |
| Key Finding | Highest dose group showed a reduction in creatine kinase-MB release (biomarker for heart damage) and a trend toward reduced infarct size, though the primary endpoint did not reach statistical significance |
| Safety | Well-tolerated across all dose groups; no dose-limiting toxicities; safety profile comparable to placebo |
