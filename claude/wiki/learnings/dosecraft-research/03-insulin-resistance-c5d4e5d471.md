# 03-insulin-resistance

Your cells have gone completely deaf to insulin.

Think of insulin as a **key** that unlocks the door on every cell in your body. When the door opens, glucose — your primary fuel — enters and gets converted to energy. This is how you stay alive.

In insulin resistance, the lock is **jammed**. Decades of processed food, seed oils, and chronic inflammation have packed the lock with intramuscular fat and inflammatory debris. Insulin keeps knocking, but the door won't open.

Your pancreas responds the only way it knows how: it **overproduces insulin** — flooding the bloodstream with more and more keys, desperately trying to force the jammed locks open. This is **hyperinsulinemia**, and it's the beginning of a catastrophic cascade.

Clinical Insulin resistance precedes Type 2 diabetes diagnosis by 10-15 years. Most doctors don't test fasting insulin — they only catch it after it becomes diabetes.

Insulin resistance is not a single disease — it's a **root cause engine** that feeds into virtually every major chronic condition. Fix this one failure, and the downstream cascade begins to unwind.

The obvious endpoint. Pancreas eventually exhausts itself. Blood sugar spirals out of control. The lock has been jammed so long the key mechanism has broken entirely.

Increasingly called **"Type 3 Diabetes"** — brain neurons become insulin resistant. Unable to absorb glucose, they literally starve to death. Cognition collapses.

Excess insulin drives androgen overproduction. Hormonal chaos ensues — irregular cycles, infertility, weight gain. The root is metabolic, not reproductive.

Hyperinsulinemia forces the liver to convert excess glucose into fat. The liver becomes a fat storage depot. Hepatic steatosis — the silent killer nobody screens for.

Glycated proteins damage artery walls. Inflammation triggers plaque formation. Hyperinsulinemia raises blood pressure. The heart pays the price for metabolic dysfunction.

Neuroinflammation fueled by insulin resistance damages myelin sheaths and neural circuits. The brain becomes progressively more energy-starved and inflamed.

Every condition above shares the same root mechanism: cells that cannot absorb glucose properly, a pancreas in overdrive, and chronic metabolic inflammation. Treat the root cause, and the downstream cascade begins to resolve.

HOMA-IR (Homeostatic Model Assessment of Insulin Resistance) is calculated as: **(Fasting Insulin x Fasting Glucose) / 405**. It quantifies how hard your pancreas is working to keep blood sugar under control.

A HOMA-IR below 1.0 means your metabolism is running clean. Above 2.5 means significant resistance is present. Most standard blood panels **do not include fasting insulin** — you must specifically request it.
