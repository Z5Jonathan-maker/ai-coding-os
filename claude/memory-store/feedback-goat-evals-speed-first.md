For prop EVAL accounts (Goat Sprint etc.), the objective is **speed-first**: minimize median days-to-pass at
acceptable bust risk. Do NOT optimize eval playbooks for "impossible to fail" / fewest red days — that's the
FUNDED-account objective (survival, consistency rules, payout cadence).

**Why:** Founder 2026-07-10: "you're trying to find playbooks that are impossible to fail. I want speed
first, at least for the goat accounts." Evals are cheap tickets (reset fee ~small); passing slowly costs
weeks of unfunded edge. A 15-day pass at 0.01% bust beats an 18-day pass at ~1 red day per 10 weeks.

**How to apply:** when constructing/judging eval books, the pre-registered bar is P(pass) + median
days-to-pass (bust risk as a constraint, not red-day frequency). Red-day minimization stays the bar for
FUNDED books only. Note this composes with the older law "never size UP evals" (aggression frontier flat —
3× size halves first-try pass): speed comes from ADDING decorrelated cells, not from sizing up. Related:
[[project-mym-best-playbook]], [[project-mym-two-parallel-challenges]].
