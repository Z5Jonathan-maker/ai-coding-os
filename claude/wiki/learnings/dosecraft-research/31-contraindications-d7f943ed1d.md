# 31-contraindications

Every compound has an edge. Knowing where that edge is — and who should not approach it — separates informed users from reckless ones.

MOTS-c activates AMPK — the cell's "metabolic CFO." In healthy mitochondria, this drives efficient fat oxidation and ATP production. But in **already-inflamed or damaged mitochondria**, AMPK overstimulation triggers an oxidative burst that *depletes* rather than generates ATP.

| Symptom | Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Crushing fatigue after injection | Oxidative burst in damaged mitochondria | Stop MOTS-c; address inflammation first (2-4 weeks) |
| Brain fog worsening | Neuronal ATP depletion from excessive AMPK | Reduce dose to 2.5mg; support with CoQ10 + B-complex |
| Muscle weakness | Skeletal muscle mitochondria overwhelmed | Pause 2 weeks; restart at 50% dose |

Expert "Prepare before you push" — see Chapter 32 for the Mitochondrial Preparedness Protocol.

As a triple GLP-1/GIP/glucagon agonist, Retatrutide produces the most powerful metabolic reset available — but its side effect profile demands respect.

| Side Effect | Severity | Mechanism | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| GI Slowdown | Common | GLP-1 slows gastric emptying dramatically | Eat smaller meals; avoid high-fat meals; ginger tea |
| Dehydration | Serious | Reduced appetite + nausea = insufficient fluid intake | Force 3-4L water daily; electrolyte supplementation mandatory |
| Electrolyte Crashes | Serious | Rapid fluid shifts; potassium/magnesium depletion | Daily electrolytes (Na, K, Mg); monitor if symptomatic |
| Temporary IR Spikes | Paradoxical | Rapid fat loss floods bloodstream with free fatty acids | Expected and transient; resolves as fat loss stabilizes |
| Nausea / Vomiting | Common | Dose-dependent GLP-1 effect on nausea centers | Titrate up SLOWLY; never microdose (defeats purpose) |

Retatrutide should NOT be combined with strict keto or zero-carb diets. The glucagon receptor agonism requires adequate carbohydrate intake to function correctly. Insufficient carbs can lead to dangerous hypoglycemic episodes and severe muscle catabolism.

Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency affects ~400 million people worldwide (higher prevalence in African, Mediterranean, and Asian populations). Methylene Blue in G6PD-deficient individuals can trigger **severe hemolytic anemia** — the destruction of red blood cells. **Screen for G6PD before EVER using Methylene Blue.**

Methylene Blue is a potent monoamine oxidase inhibitor (MAOI). Combining it with SSRIs (fluoxetine, sertraline, etc.) or SNRIs (venlafaxine, duloxetine) can trigger **serotonin syndrome** — a potentially fatal condition causing hyperthermia, seizures, and autonomic instability. **Never combine with serotonergic medications.**

Methylene Blue follows a hormetic curve: **low dose (0.5-1mg) = beneficial**, high dose (10-15mg+) = toxic. Continuous use without cycling leads to accumulation and mitochondrial damage — the opposite of its intended effect. Cycle: 5 days on, 2 days off minimum.

PT-141 activates melanocortin receptors (MC3R and MC4R) in the brain to enhance sexual desire and arousal. However, it has a sharp dose-response ceiling — exceeding it produces severe side effects with no additional benefit.

| Dose Range | Effect | Side Effects |
|---|---|---|
| 0.5-1.5mg | Optimal therapeutic range; enhanced arousal within 45-60 min | Mild facial flushing (expected and transient) |
| 1.5-2.0mg | Diminishing returns; ceiling of effect | Moderate nausea, pronounced flushing |
| 2.0mg+ | No additional benefit; side effects dominate | Severe nausea, vomiting, significant flushing ("look like a tomato"), headache, blood pressure changes |

Expert Use as-needed only, not daily. Maximum frequency: 2-3 times per week. Start at 0.5mg.

Growth hormone naturally antagonizes insulin. GH secretagogues (Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin) elevate GH and IGF-1. If your baseline fasting insulin is already elevated (above 8 mIU/L), these compounds push you **further into metabolic dysfunction**.

| Fasting Insulin | GH Secretagogue Safety | Action |
|---|---|---|
| < 5 mIU/L | Safe | Proceed with standard dosing and monitoring |
| 5-8 mIU/L | Caution | Address IR first with MOTS-c or lifestyle; retest before starting GH compounds |
| > 8 mIU/L | Contraindicated | Resolve insulin resistance BEFORE any GH secretagogue protocol |

Progesterone cream can **blunt the effects of GH secretagogues** if taken at the same time. Separate administration by at least 4-6 hours. Best practice: GH secretagogues in the morning fasted; progesterone cream in the evening.

| Compound | Interacts With | Severity | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Methylene Blue | SSRIs / SNRIs / MAOIs | SEVERE | Serotonin syndrome — potentially fatal |
| GH Secretagogues | Insulin / Metformin | MODERATE | Opposing metabolic effects; monitor glucose closely |
| GH Secretagogues | Progesterone cream | MODERATE | Blunted GH response; separate by 4-6 hours |
| Retatrutide | Semaglutide / Tirzepatide (any GLP-1 agonist) | CONTRAINDICATED | NEVER combine GLP-1 agonists — pancreatitis & gastroparesis risk |
| Semaglutide | Tirzepatide / Retatrutide (any GLP-1 agonist) | CONTRAINDICATED | NEVER combine GLP-1 agonists — pancreatitis & gastroparesis risk |
| Retatrutide | Oral medications | MODERATE | Delayed absorption from slowed gastric emptying |
| Retatrutide | Insulin / Sulfonylureas | SEVERE | Hypoglycemia risk; requires dose adjustment |
| PT-141 | Blood pressure medications | MODERATE | Additive hypotension; monitor BP |
| KPV | Immunosuppressants | MODERATE | Conflicting immune modulation; consult prescriber |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 | Checkpoint inhibitors | MODERATE | Additive immune activation; oncologist supervision required |

These are non-negotiable. If any apply, do NOT use the specified compound without direct medical supervision:

| Condition | Contraindicated Compounds | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| G6PD Deficiency | Methylene Blue | Hemolytic anemia — red blood cell destruction |
| Active SSRI/SNRI Use | Methylene Blue | Serotonin syndrome — potentially fatal |
| GLP-1 Agonist Stacking | Any combination of Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Retatrutide | Pancreatitis & gastroparesis — NEVER combine GLP-1 agonists |
| Active Cancer | All GH secretagogues; all peptides (precautionary) | Theoretical growth promotion; consult oncologist |
| Pregnancy / Nursing | ALL peptides and compounds | No safety data; fetal/neonatal risk unknown |
| Fasting Insulin > 8 | GH secretagogues (Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin) | Worsens insulin resistance |
| Inflamed Mitochondria | MOTS-c (until prepared) | Paradoxical fatigue from AMPK overstimulation |

A critical point from clinical practitioners: peptides do not create dependency, drop-off effects, or addiction. When you stop a peptide, you return to baseline — you do not go below it. There is no withdrawal, no rebound, no "crash." The benefits accumulate during use and gradually diminish after cessation. This is fundamentally different from hormonal therapies or pharmaceutical interventions.

These compound-condition pairings are non-negotiable. No exceptions without direct specialist supervision.

| Condition | Contraindicated Compounds | Mechanism / Risk |
|---|---|---|
| G6PD Deficiency | Methylene Blue | Hemolytic anemia — destruction of red blood cells; potentially fatal |
| Active SSRI/SNRI/MAOI Use | Methylene Blue | Serotonin syndrome — hyperthermia, seizures, death |
| Active Cancer (any type) | All GH secretagogues (Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin, MK-677), IGF-1-elevating compounds | Growth factors can accelerate tumor growth via IGF-1/mTOR pathways |
| Active Cancer | BPC-157 (precautionary) | VEGF upregulation may promote tumor angiogenesis (theoretical) |
| GLP-1 Agonist Already Prescribed | Retatrutide, Semaglutide, Tirzepatide (any second GLP-1) | Stacking GLP-1 agonists = pancreatitis and gastroparesis risk |
| Pregnancy / Breastfeeding | ALL peptides and compounds in this book | No safety data exists; fetal/neonatal risk unknown |
| Fasting Insulin >8 mIU/L | GH secretagogues | GH antagonizes insulin; worsens metabolic dysfunction |
| Personal History of Pancreatitis | All GLP-1 agonists (Retatrutide, Semaglutide, Tirzepatide) | GLP-1 agonists carry documented pancreatitis risk |
| Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma (personal or family hx) | All GLP-1 agonists | GLP-1 receptor agonism linked to thyroid C-cell tumors in animal models |

Not absolute prohibitions, but require careful monitoring, dose adjustment, or specialist consultation.

| Condition | Compounds Requiring Caution | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Cancer in Remission (>5 years) | GH secretagogues, BPC-157 | Oncologist clearance required; monitor IGF-1 closely; avoid if tumor was GH-responsive |
| Type 1 Diabetes | MOTS-c, GH secretagogues, Retatrutide | Insulin requirements may change; endocrinologist monitoring mandatory |
| Autoimmune Conditions (active) | Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV | Immune modulation could worsen or improve; start at lowest dose; rheumatologist aware |
| Liver Disease / Elevated Enzymes | All oral compounds (MB, 5-Amino-1MQ, NMN) | Liver processes oral compounds; get hepatologist clearance if ALT/AST >2x ULN |
| Kidney Disease (eGFR <60) | All injectable peptides | Peptides cleared renally; dose adjustments may be needed; nephrologist input |
| Under 25 Years of Age | GH secretagogues, Epitalon | Endocrine system still developing; GH axis intervention premature in most cases |

| Drug Class | Interacting Compound(s) | Severity | Effect & Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blood Thinners (Warfarin, Heparin, Apixaban, Rivaroxaban) | BPC-157, TB-500 | MODERATE | BPC-157 modulates coagulation pathways. Monitor INR if on warfarin. Increased bruising at injection sites. Inform prescriber. |
| Immunosuppressants (Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine, Mycophenolate) | Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV | SEVERE | Immune modulators conflict with immunosuppression. Transplant patients: NEVER use without transplant team approval. Risk of rejection. |
| Diabetes Medications (Insulin, Metformin, Sulfonylureas) | MOTS-c, Retatrutide, GH secretagogues | SEVERE | Hypoglycemia risk with sulfonylureas/insulin. MOTS-c + metformin may have additive effect (monitor). Retatrutide requires dose adjustment of existing diabetes meds. |
| Corticosteroids (Prednisone, Dexamethasone) | BPC-157, MOTS-c, Thymosin Alpha-1 | MODERATE | Corticosteroids suppress immune/inflammatory responses that peptides are trying to modulate. Conflicting mechanisms reduce efficacy. |
| Checkpoint Inhibitors (Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab) | Thymosin Alpha-1 | SEVERE | Additive immune activation. Risk of cytokine storm or autoimmune flare. Oncologist supervision mandatory. |
| Antihypertensives (ACE inhibitors, Beta-blockers) | PT-141 | MODERATE | Additive hypotension. Monitor blood pressure. Start PT-141 at lower dose (0.5 mg). |

**ALL peptides, SARMs, and compounds discussed in this Protocol Bible are contraindicated during pregnancy, breastfeeding, and while actively trying to conceive.**

No human safety data exists for peptide use during gestation or lactation. Peptides cross biological barriers, modulate growth factors (VEGF, IGF-1, TGF-beta), alter immune function, and affect hormonal signaling — all systems critical to fetal development. The risk of teratogenic effects, developmental abnormalities, or neonatal harm is unknown and therefore assumed to be present.

**Discontinue ALL peptides at least 4 weeks before planned conception.** If you discover you are pregnant while on a protocol, discontinue immediately and inform your obstetrician of all compounds used, with dates and dosages.

Growth factors are the critical concern. Any compound that upregulates IGF-1, VEGF, or mTOR signaling can theoretically promote the growth of dormant cancer cells or micrometastases.

| Cancer History Status | Avoid These Compounds | Potentially Acceptable (with oncologist clearance) |
|---|---|---|
| Active cancer | ALL peptides — no exceptions | None |
| Remission <5 years | GH secretagogues, BPC-157 (VEGF concern), RAD-140 | Thymosin Alpha-1 (immune surveillance), KPV (anti-inflammatory), Methylene Blue |
| Remission >5 years | GH secretagogues (still caution), RAD-140 | BPC-157, MOTS-c, Epitalon, Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV, Methylene Blue (all with monitoring) |

**Key principle:** Thymosin Alpha-1 is the most cancer-compatible peptide in this book — it enhances immune surveillance against cancer cells rather than promoting growth. Some oncologists actively use it as adjunctive therapy. Always get oncologist clearance regardless.
