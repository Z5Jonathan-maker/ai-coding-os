# 16-supporting-compounds

Antimicrobial & Mitochondrial
## Immune Defense

& Mitochondrial Protection

LL-37 represents the innate immune system's first line of peptide defense, while SS-31 targets the inner mitochondrial membrane — two entirely different biological domains, both critical for resilience.

The only cathelicidin antimicrobial peptide found in humans. LL-37 is a 37-amino-acid peptide that disrupts bacterial cell membranes, penetrates and dissolves biofilms, and modulates the innate immune response. Biofilms — structured bacterial communities shielded from antibiotics — are implicated in chronic infections, Lyme disease, and persistent inflammatory states. LL-37 attacks these biofilms at a structural level that conventional antibiotics cannot reach.

- Broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity (bacteria, fungi, viruses)
- Biofilm disruption and penetration
- Immunomodulation (recruits immune cells, modulates cytokines)
- Wound healing acceleration
- Anti-inflammatory properties at appropriate doses

**Dose:** 50–100 mcg/day

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 4–8 weeks (infection-dependent)

**Timing:** Morning

Synergies:
Thymosin Alpha-1 (immune axis)
BPC-157 (tissue repair)
KPV (anti-inflammatory)

Contraindications:
Active autoimmune flare (may over-stimulate)
Start low, titrate up

The body's own biofilm-buster. When chronic infections persist despite antibiotics, biofilm disruption is often the missing piece. LL-37 addresses this gap directly. Essential in Lyme, chronic sinusitis, and persistent infection protocols.

A synthetic hexapeptide that stimulates growth hormone release by activating the ghrelin receptor (GHS-R1a) in the pituitary. GHRP-2 produces the strongest GH pulse among the GHRP family — approximately 2–3x more potent than GHRP-6 — with moderate appetite stimulation. Works synergistically with GHRH analogs for amplified, pulsatile GH release that mimics youthful secretion patterns.

- Potent GH release via ghrelin receptor activation
- Improved sleep quality and deep sleep architecture
- Enhanced fat metabolism and lean body composition
- Accelerated injury recovery and tissue repair

**Dose:** 100–300 mcg per injection

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 12–16 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Pre-bed or upon waking (fasted, no carbs 30 min before/after)

Synergies:
CJC-1295
Ipamorelin
Sermorelin

Contraindications:
Active cancer
May elevate cortisol and prolactin at high doses

The strongest GHRP for raw GH output. Best paired with a GHRH analog (CJC-1295 or Sermorelin) to amplify the pulse 3–5x beyond either peptide alone. Use Ipamorelin instead if cortisol/prolactin sensitivity is a concern.

A first-generation GHRP that activates the ghrelin receptor to trigger pulsatile GH release. Notable for significant appetite stimulation — a feature that can be beneficial for underweight individuals or those in caloric surplus phases. Produces reliable GH pulses but with more cortisol and prolactin elevation than newer GHRPs like Ipamorelin.

- Reliable GH secretion via ghrelin pathway
- Strong appetite stimulation (useful for bulking phases)
- Gastric motility improvement and gut healing properties
- Neuroprotective effects demonstrated in animal models

**Dose:** 100–300 mcg per injection

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 12–16 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Pre-bed or post-workout (fasted state)

Synergies:
CJC-1295
Sermorelin

Contraindications:
Active cancer
Intense hunger spikes — problematic for fat loss phases

The original GHRP workhorse. Largely superseded by Ipamorelin and GHRP-2 for most use cases, but remains valuable when appetite stimulation is desired. Best for hard-gainers or recovery-focused protocols.

The most potent GHRP available, producing the largest single-dose GH release among all ghrelin-receptor agonists. Unique among GHRPs for direct cardioprotective effects — it activates cardiac GHS receptors independent of GH release, improving left ventricular function and reducing cardiac fibrosis. However, it desensitizes rapidly and significantly elevates cortisol and prolactin.

- Strongest acute GH release of any GHRP
- Direct cardioprotective effects via cardiac GHS receptors
- Anti-fibrotic activity in cardiac tissue
- Improved left ventricular ejection fraction in clinical studies

**Dose:** 100–200 mcg per injection

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 4–8 weeks max (desensitization occurs)

**Timing:** Pre-bed (fasted)

Synergies:
CJC-1295
Sermorelin

Contraindications:
Rapid desensitization — short cycles only
Significant cortisol and prolactin elevation

Maximum GH firepower with a unique cardiac benefit — but the desensitization problem limits it to short bursts. Best used as a 4-week "blast" within a broader GH protocol, or specifically for cardiac recovery applications.

A truncated analog of endogenous GHRH (first 29 amino acids) that stimulates the pituitary to release GH in a physiologic, pulsatile pattern. Unlike exogenous GH, Sermorelin preserves the hypothalamic-pituitary feedback loop — the body retains its own regulatory mechanisms. FDA-approved for pediatric GH deficiency and widely used off-label in anti-aging protocols.

- Physiologic pulsatile GH release (preserves feedback loop)
- Improved sleep quality and REM architecture
- Enhanced recovery, skin quality, and body composition
- FDA-approved with extensive safety profile

**Dose:** 200–500 mcg/day

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 3–6 months continuous

**Timing:** Pre-bed (30 min before sleep, fasted)

Synergies:
Ipamorelin
GHRP-2
MK-677

Contraindications:
Active cancer
Less potent than CJC-1295 with DAC — requires daily dosing

The gentlest on-ramp to GH optimization. Ideal for GH beginners or those who want physiologic restoration without aggressive stimulation. Pair with Ipamorelin for the classic "clean GH stack" with minimal side effects.

A modified form of IGF-1 with an extended half-life (~20–30 hours vs. ~15 minutes for native IGF-1). The arginine substitution at position 3 reduces binding protein affinity, keeping more IGF-1 bioavailable. Drives hyperplasia (new muscle cell formation) rather than just hypertrophy — a unique mechanism not achievable with GH or testosterone alone.

- Muscle hyperplasia (new fiber creation, not just enlargement)
- Systemic anabolic effects with extended bioavailability
- Enhanced nitrogen retention and protein synthesis
- Improved nutrient partitioning toward lean tissue

**Dose:** 20–50 mcg/day

**Route:** SubQ or IM injection

**Cycle:** 4–6 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Post-workout or morning

Synergies:
GH / GH Peptides
MGF (localized growth)

Contraindications:
Active cancer or tumor history
Hypoglycemia risk — monitor blood glucose
Short cycles only — organ growth risk with extended use

The only compound that drives true muscle hyperplasia. Powerful but demands respect — strict cycle limits, glucose monitoring, and cancer screening are non-negotiable. Not a beginner compound.

A truncated form of IGF-1 missing the first three N-terminal amino acids. This modification makes it 10x more potent than native IGF-1 but with a very short half-life (~20–30 minutes). The brief activity window enables precise, site-specific muscle growth when injected directly into target tissue — ideal for bringing up lagging muscle groups.

- 10x binding potency vs. native IGF-1
- Site-specific hyperplasia when injected locally
- Rapid clearance minimizes systemic side effects
- Targeted muscle group development

**Dose:** 20–50 mcg per site

**Route:** IM injection (into target muscle)

**Cycle:** 4 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Immediately pre-workout (into target muscle)

Synergies:
IGF-1 LR3 (systemic + local)
PEG-MGF

Contraindications:
Active cancer
Hypoglycemia risk with concurrent insulin use

The precision tool for site-specific muscle growth. Where IGF-1 LR3 works systemically, DES works locally. Advanced compound for experienced users targeting specific weak points.

A naturally occurring glycoprotein that binds and neutralizes myostatin — the body's primary muscle growth limiter. By removing the myostatin "brake," Follistatin 344 unlocks muscle growth potential beyond normal physiological limits. Also inhibits activin A, reducing inflammation and fibrosis. The 344-amino acid isoform is the most bioavailable injectable form.

- Myostatin neutralization — removes muscle growth ceiling
- Enhanced lean muscle mass independent of androgen signaling
- Anti-fibrotic and anti-inflammatory via activin inhibition
- Potential fertility benefits (FSH modulation)

**Dose:** 100–200 mcg/day

**Route:** SubQ injection

**Cycle:** 10–30 days (short bursts)

**Timing:** Morning or post-workout

Synergies:
IGF-1 LR3
GH Peptides

Contraindications:
Active cancer (growth factor amplification)
Limited human data — predominantly animal studies

The myostatin "off switch." Theoretically the most powerful anabolic mechanism available — but human data remains limited. Short cycles recommended until more clinical evidence accumulates. Experimental compound for informed users.

A splice variant of IGF-1 produced naturally in muscle tissue after mechanical loading (exercise). MGF activates satellite cells — the dormant stem cells within muscle fibers — initiating the repair and growth cascade. Native MGF has a very short half-life (~minutes), requiring either PEGylation or immediate post-workout local injection for practical use.

- Satellite cell activation for muscle repair
- Enhanced post-exercise recovery cascade
- Localized tissue repair when injected at injury site
- Synergistic with IGF-1 LR3 for complete growth signaling

**Dose:** 100–200 mcg per site

**Route:** IM injection (into trained muscle)

**Cycle:** 4 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Immediately post-workout

Synergies:
IGF-1 LR3 (systemic layer)
PEG-MGF (extended version)

Contraindications:
Active cancer
Ultra-short half-life limits practical utility without PEGylation

The body's natural post-workout repair signal, isolated and amplified. Best used as the localized complement to systemic IGF-1 LR3. Most users prefer PEG-MGF for convenience, but native MGF offers more precise site targeting.

PEGylated version of MGF with a polyethylene glycol chain attached to extend the half-life from minutes to several hours. This modification allows systemic distribution and less frequent dosing compared to native MGF. Activates satellite cells throughout the body rather than just at the injection site, providing a broader recovery and growth stimulus.

- Extended half-life (hours vs. minutes for native MGF)
- Systemic satellite cell activation
- Less frequent dosing schedule (2–3x per week)
- Enhanced whole-body recovery from training

**Dose:** 200–400 mcg, 2–3x per week

**Route:** SubQ or IM injection

**Cycle:** 4–6 weeks on, 4 weeks off

**Timing:** Post-workout on training days, morning on rest days

Synergies:
IGF-1 LR3
GH Peptides

Contraindications:
Active cancer
Predominantly animal data — limited human trials

The practical version of MGF. PEGylation solves the half-life problem, enabling systemic recovery benefits with a manageable dosing schedule. Standard choice for growth factor stacks alongside IGF-1 LR3.

A long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist that revolutionized obesity treatment. Mimics the incretin hormone GLP-1 to suppress appetite centrally (hypothalamus), slow gastric emptying, and enhance insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner. FDA-approved as Ozempic (diabetes) and Wegovy (obesity). Produces 15–20% body weight loss in clinical trials — unprecedented for a pharmaceutical intervention.

- 15–20% total body weight loss in clinical trials
- Significant cardiovascular risk reduction (MACE)
- Improved glycemic control and insulin sensitivity
- Reduced food noise and appetite centrally

**Dose:** 0.25 mg/week → titrate to 2.4 mg/week over 16–20 weeks

**Route:** SubQ injection (weekly)

**Cycle:** Continuous (weight regain upon cessation)

**Timing:** Same day each week, any time

Synergies:
Resistance training (preserve lean mass)
High protein intake (≥1g/lb)

Contraindications:
Personal/family MTC or MEN2 history
Pancreatitis history
GI side effects common during titration (nausea, constipation)

The most impactful metabolic drug of the decade. Unparalleled weight loss efficacy backed by massive Phase III data. Must be paired with resistance training and high protein to prevent lean mass loss. Slow titration is essential to manage GI side effects.

A first-in-class dual incretin agonist that activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors simultaneously. This dual mechanism produces superior weight loss (up to 22.5% in SURMOUNT trials) compared to GLP-1-only agonists. FDA-approved as Mounjaro (diabetes) and Zepbound (obesity). The GIP component may provide additional benefits for bone density and fat redistribution that GLP-1 alone does not.

- Up to 22.5% body weight loss (SURMOUNT-1 trial)
- Superior glycemic control vs. GLP-1 monotherapy
- Dual incretin pathway activation
- Potential bone density preservation via GIP receptor

**Dose:** 2.5 mg/week → titrate to 15 mg/week over 20 weeks

**Route:** SubQ injection (weekly)

The next evolution beyond Semaglutide. Dual incretin action produces the largest pharmaceutical weight loss ever recorded. Same lean-mass preservation requirements apply — resistance training and protein are non-negotiable companions.

An adenosine analog that directly activates AMPK — the master metabolic sensor that normally activates during exercise. AICAR triggers the same metabolic adaptations as endurance training: increased fatty acid oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, GLUT4 translocation, and enhanced insulin sensitivity. Often called the "exercise in a bottle" compound, though this oversimplifies its mechanism.

- Direct AMPK activation (exercise-mimetic signaling)
- Enhanced fatty acid oxidation and glucose uptake
- Mitochondrial biogenesis stimulation

The purest AMPK activator available. Fascinating mechanism but impractical for most due to extreme cost and injection volume. Cardarine provides similar endurance benefits at a fraction of the cost and with oral dosing.

A triple reuptake inhibitor of norepinephrine, dopamine, and serotonin originally developed for Parkinson's and Alzheimer's. Serendipitously discovered to produce significant weight loss (10–13% in Phase II trials) by suppressing appetite and increasing resting metabolic rate. Works through central monoamine pathways rather than the incretin axis, making it mechanistically complementary to GLP-1 agonists.

- 10–13% weight loss in Phase II trials
- Appetite suppression via central monoamine pathways

A different weight loss mechanism than GLP-1 agonists — attacks the problem through monoamine pathways. Phase II data is strong but Phase III is still underway. Contraindicated with existing antidepressants. Promising but not yet fully validated.

A once-weekly GLP-1 receptor agonist (brand name Trulicity) with proven cardiovascular benefits. Uses a modified GLP-1 analog fused to an IgG4 Fc fragment for extended half-life. While producing less weight loss than Semaglutide (~5–7%), it demonstrated significant MACE reduction in the REWIND trial and has a well-established safety profile from years of clinical use.

- Proven cardiovascular risk reduction (REWIND trial)
- Reliable glycemic control with weekly dosing

The cardiovascular-first GLP-1 agonist. Choose Dulaglutide when the primary goal is cardioprotection with moderate metabolic benefit, not maximum weight loss. Longest real-world safety track record in the GLP-1 class.

The first GLP-1 receptor agonist approved (2005), derived from exendin-4 — a peptide found in Gila monster saliva. Available as twice-daily (Byetta) or once-weekly (Bydureon) formulations. While superseded by Semaglutide and Tirzepatide for weight loss, Exenatide retains relevance for its beta-cell preservation data and neuroprotective properties currently being investigated for Parkinson's disease.

- Proven beta-cell preservation and regeneration signals
- Neuroprotective properties (Parkinson's Phase II data)

The grandfather of GLP-1 therapy. No longer first-line for weight loss, but uniquely interesting for its beta-cell and neuroprotective data. Consider when Parkinson's risk or beta-cell preservation is the priority over maximal weight loss.

A macrolide antibiotic that inhibits mTORC1 — the master growth/aging switch. Originally discovered on Easter Island and used as an immunosuppressant in organ transplant. At low, intermittent doses, Rapamycin activates autophagy, reduces cellular senescence, and extends lifespan in every organism tested (yeast to mammals). The most robust pharmacological lifespan extension ever documented in preclinical models.

- Lifespan extension in every model organism tested

The single most evidence-backed longevity compound in existence. Low-dose weekly protocols appear to rejuvenate rather than suppress the immune system. Requires a prescriber familiar with longevity dosing — transplant doses are 5–10x higher and immunosuppressive. Regular bloodwork is mandatory.

The best natural AMPK activator available. Clinical data rivals metformin for glycemic control with bonus lipid benefits. Excellent option for those who prefer or cannot tolerate pharmaceutical metabolic agents. Do not stack with metformin.

The foundation of every detox and longevity protocol. Declining glutathione is one of the most universal markers of aging. Liposomal oral or IV routes are the only effective delivery methods — standard oral capsules are largely destroyed in the GI tract.

Restores thymic function and T-cell maturation — directly countering thymic involution, one of the primary drivers of immune aging. Landmark clinical studies showed a 2x reduction in mortality over a 6-year follow-up in elderly patients treated with Thymalin + Epithalon. The thymic rejuvenation peptide with the longest clinical track record. The mortality reduction data is remarkable if validated by Western standards. Pair with Epithalon for the classic Khavinson longevity protocol. Twice-yearly 10-day courses are the standard approach.

Thymulin is the only thymic hormone that is a true circulating hormone with measurable serum levels. It promotes T-cell differentiation, modulates cytokine production, and possesses analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The thymus gland's own circulating hormone. Unique among thymic peptides for its zinc dependency — supplementing Thymulin without adequate zinc is ineffective. Interesting analgesic properties make it relevant beyond pure immune applications.

Developed in Russia with clinical approval for cognitive impairment. Acts rapidly (within 15–20 minutes) with effects on memory consolidation, learning, and focus.

Designed as a more targeted, injectable nootropic that specifically drives new neuron formation in memory-critical brain regions. Penetrates the blood-brain barrier efficiently.

The cortex-derived counterpart to Cerebrolysin. Extensive Russian clinical use for neurorehabilitation with a strong safety record. Western validation is limited but the neuroprotective mechanism is well-characterized. Standard 10-day courses provide lasting cognitive benefits.

A lipopeptide that mimics the appearance of broken-down collagen fragments (matrikines), tricking fibroblasts into producing new collagen, elastin, and hyaluronic acid. Clinical studies show wrinkle depth reduction comparable to retinol without the irritation. The palmitoyl chain enhances skin penetration and cell membrane interaction. One of the most well-validated cosmetic peptides in dermatological literature.

- Stimulates collagen I, III, and IV synthesis
- Increases elastin and hyaluronic acid production
- Wrinkle depth reduction comparable to retinol
- Non-irritating — suitable for sensitive skin

The gold standard collagen-stimulating cosmetic peptide. Strong clinical evidence for wrinkle reduction without retinol-level irritation. Best results after 8–12 weeks of consistent use. Pair with Argireline for a multi-mechanism anti-aging topical stack.

A hexapeptide that inhibits SNARE complex formation — the same mechanism as botulinum toxin — to reduce neuromuscular contraction at the skin surface. Reduces expression wrinkles (forehead lines, crow's feet) by up to 30% in clinical studies. Unlike Botox, Argireline is non-toxic, non-injectable, and works gradually with topical application. Often called "topical Botox" though the effect is subtler.

- Expression wrinkle reduction up to 30%
- SNARE complex inhibition (Botox-like mechanism, topically)
- Non-invasive — no injections required
- Safe for daily long-term use

The non-invasive Botox alternative. Clinically validated for expression wrinkle reduction. Results are meaningful but more gradual and subtle than injectable neurotoxins. Stack with SNAP-8 for enhanced SNARE complex inhibition and Matrixyl for collagen building.

An octapeptide extension of Argireline that provides enhanced SNARE complex inhibition.
