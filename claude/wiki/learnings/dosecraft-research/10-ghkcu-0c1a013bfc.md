# 10-ghkcu

"The Finisher / Quality Control"

A naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide that finishes what other peptides start — cleaning oxidative stress, remodeling tissue, and restoring skin, nerves, and blood vessels to their original quality.

The third compound in the **Wolverine Protocol** — the quality control inspector that ensures every repair job is finished to the highest standard.

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring tripeptide (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine) that binds to copper ions. Present in human plasma, saliva, and urine, its concentration declines dramatically with age — from 200 ng/mL at age 20 to 80 ng/mL by age 60.

GHK-Cu's tripeptide structure chelates copper(II) ions with high affinity. Copper is essential for dozens of enzymatic processes — from collagen crosslinking to superoxide dismutase (antioxidant defense). The peptide delivers copper precisely where repair is needed.

ClinicalResearch shows GHK-Cu modulates the expression of over 4,000 human genes — upregulating repair genes and downregulating inflammatory and tissue destruction genes. This makes it one of the most broadly acting regenerative compounds known.
