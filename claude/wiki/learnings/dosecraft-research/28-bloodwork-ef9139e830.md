# 28-bloodwork

The complete marker panel, optimal ranges, testing frequency, and a progress journal template. Because a short pencil is better than a long memory.

You cannot optimize what you do not measure. But you also should not be getting blood drawn every seven minutes. This chapter strikes the right balance.

These markers map directly to the Three Root Causes framework. Every marker tells you which biological failure to prioritize.

| Marker | Optimal Range | Root Cause | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|---|
| CRP | < 1.0 mg/L | Inflammation | Systemic inflammation; >3.0 = active fire |
| Homocysteine | < 8 umol/L | Inflammation | Cardiovascular risk + methylation status |
| IL-6 | < 1.8 pg/mL | Inflammation | Pro-inflammatory cytokine level |
| Ferritin | 40-150 ng/mL | Inflammation | Iron storage; elevated = hidden inflammation |
| Fasting Insulin | < 5 mIU/L | Insulin Resistance | Primary IR indicator; >8 = significant |
| HbA1c | < 5.3% | Insulin Resistance | 3-month blood sugar average |
| HOMA-IR | < 1.0 | Insulin Resistance | Gold standard IR calculation |
| IGF-1 | 150-250 ng/mL | ATP / Hormones | Growth hormone axis function |
| DHEA-S | Age-appropriate upper quartile | ATP / Hormones | Adrenal function, aging marker |

Clinical Expert Optimal ranges differ from "normal" lab ranges. Lab "normal" includes sick populations. These are optimized health ranges from practitioner protocols.

Get these drawn **before your first injection**. This is non-negotiable. Without a baseline, you cannot measure progress, identify contraindications, or adjust protocols intelligently.

If starting a new compound or stack, an **abbreviated 8-week recheck** catches early safety signals. Not a full panel — focused on the markers most likely to shift with peptide use:

| Marker | Why at 8 Weeks | What to Watch For |
|---|---|---|
| Fasting Insulin | MOTS-c, GH secretagogues, Retatrutide affect insulin signaling | Should be trending down or stable |
| IGF-1 | GH secretagogues, Epitalon may elevate | Flag if >300 ng/mL (excessive GH stimulation) |
| hs-CRP | All peptides should reduce; rising CRP = problem | Flag if rising from baseline |
| ALT / AST | Liver safety check for any oral compound (MB, 5-Amino, NMN) | Flag if >2x upper normal limit |
| CBC | Methylene Blue can affect RBC parameters in G6PD carriers | Flag any hemoglobin or hematocrit drop |

You do not need blood drawn every week. You do not need a wellness clinic checking your markers "every seven minutes." Six-month intervals for objective bloodwork, combined with weekly subjective tracking, gives you the complete picture.

| Timeframe | What to Do | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Before starting protocols | Full baseline panel (all markers above) | Establishes your starting point; identifies dominant root cause |
| 6 months into protocol | Repeat full panel | Measures objective changes; validates or redirects your approach |
| Every 6 months ongoing | Repeat full panel | Long-term trending; catches new issues early |
| Weekly (ongoing) | Subjective journal (pain, sleep, energy, mood) | Catches changes bloodwork misses; tracks daily response |

Direct-to-consumer labs let you order your own panels without a doctor visit: **Marek Health, Quest Diagnostics (direct), LabCorp (direct), Ulta Lab Tests, Jason Health, InsideTracker**. Prices range from $75-250 for a comprehensive panel. No wellness clinic markup required.

CRP > 3.0, homocysteine > 12, IL-6 elevated, ferritin > 200

| Priority Compounds | Action |
|---|---|
| BPC-157 (500mcg-1mg/day) | Systemic anti-inflammatory; start immediately |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 (1mg/day) | Immune recalibration — train T-cells |
| KPV (4-6 week cycle) | NF-kB inhibition for deep inflammation |

Fasting insulin > 8, HbA1c > 5.5, HOMA-IR > 1.5

| Priority Compounds | Action |
|---|---|
| MOTS-c (5-10mg per week total) | AMPK activation; insulin sensitizer |
| 5-Amino-1MQ (50-100mg/day oral) | NAD+ amplification; fat metabolism |
| Retatrutide (titrate per protocol) | Triple agonist for severe IR + obesity |

If fasting insulin is above 8 mIU/L, do NOT start GH secretagogues (Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, Tesamorelin). Growth hormone antagonizes insulin. Fix insulin resistance FIRST.

IGF-1 below 150, DHEA low for age, crushing fatigue with normal inflammation and insulin

| Priority Compounds | Action |
|---|---|
| MOTS-c (mitochondrial optimization) | Build ATP production capacity |
| Methylene Blue (0.5-1mg oral, cycled) | Bypass electron transport bottleneck |
| Epitalon (500mcg-1mg/day x 10-20 days) | Cellular renewal and circadian reset |

| Compound | Primary Markers Affected | Optimal Response | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| BPC-157 | hs-CRP, IL-6, ESR | CRP declining toward <1.0 | CRP rising = vendor quality issue or infection |
| MOTS-c | Fasting Insulin, HbA1c, HOMA-IR, triglycerides | Insulin dropping toward <5; HbA1c <5.3% | Crushing fatigue = mitochondria unprepared (Ch. 32) |
| Thymosin Alpha-1 | CBC with differential, NK cell count, T-cell subsets | Improved lymphocyte ratios; normalized WBC | Elevated WBC = investigate infection |
| Epitalon | Melatonin (serum), IGF-1, cortisol (AM), hs-CRP | Melatonin peak improving; CRP stable/down | IGF-1 >300 = excessive growth stimulation |
| GH Secretagogues | IGF-1, fasting insulin, fasting glucose | IGF-1 in 200–280 range; insulin stable | Insulin rising = GH antagonizing insulin; pause |
| Methylene Blue | CBC (hemoglobin, hematocrit, reticulocytes), ALT, AST | All stable; cognitive improvement subjective | Hgb drop = possible hemolysis; stop immediately |
| Retatrutide | Fasting insulin, HbA1c, lipid panel, amylase, lipase | Insulin/HbA1c improving; lipids normalizing | Amylase/lipase >3x ULN = pancreatitis risk; stop |
| KPV | hs-CRP, IL-6, calprotectin (if GI inflammation) | Inflammatory markers declining | No change after 4 weeks = review protocol |

Expert Always interpret results in context of your protocol. A single marker out of range is noise. A consistent trend across 2–3 draws is a signal.
