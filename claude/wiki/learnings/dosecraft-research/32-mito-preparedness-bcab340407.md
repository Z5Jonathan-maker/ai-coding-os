# 32-mito-preparedness

"Prepare before you push." Your mitochondria need a foundation of support before you ask them to perform at a higher level.

2-4 weeks of essential supplements to rebuild mitochondrial infrastructure

Low-dose peptide introduction to test mitochondrial response

Ramp to full therapeutic dose based on response

These are the raw materials your mitochondria need to function. Without them, peptides like MOTS-c are trying to tune an engine with no oil.

| Supplement | Dose | Role | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| CoQ10 (Ubiquinol) | 200-400mg/day | Electron transport chain cofactor; essential for Complex I and III | Critical |
| Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) | 2-4g/day | Mitochondrial membrane integrity; anti-inflammatory resolvin production | Critical |
| Creatine Monohydrate | 5g/day | ATP buffer system; phosphocreatine shuttle | Critical |
| Magnesium (Glycinate) | 400-600mg/day | Required cofactor for 300+ enzymatic reactions including ATP synthesis | Critical |
| B-Complex (Methylated) | 1 capsule/day | B1, B2, B3 are direct ETC cofactors; B12/folate for methylation | High |
| Zinc | 15-30mg/day | Antioxidant defense (SOD cofactor); immune function | High |
| Vitamin D3 + K2 | 5,000 IU D3 / 200mcg K2 | Gene expression regulation; calcium metabolism; immune modulation | High |

If your CRP is above 3.0 mg/L, you must address **Root Cause #1 (Inflammation) before Root Cause #3 (ATP)**. Inflamed mitochondria cannot be optimized — they must be de-inflamed first. Start with BPC-157, KPV, or Thymosin Alpha-1 for 4-6 weeks, retest CRP, then proceed to mitochondrial compounds.

Start MOTS-c at 50% of target dose. Monitor closely for paradoxical fatigue.

| Week | MOTS-c Dose | Frequency | Monitor For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 3 | 2.5mg SubQ | 2x per week | Energy levels 4-24 hours post-injection; any fatigue worsening |
| Week 4 | 5mg SubQ | 2x per week | Sustained energy; exercise performance; sleep quality changes |

STOP MOTS-c immediately. Return to Phase 1 foundation supplements for an additional 2-4 weeks. Consider adding BPC-157 (500mcg/day) to accelerate mitochondrial membrane repair. Retest CRP. Do not retry MOTS-c until fatigue has fully resolved.

If Phase 2 was tolerated well — energy improved, no adverse reactions — advance to full therapeutic dosing.

| Week | MOTS-c Dose | Frequency | Expected Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 5-6 | 5mg SubQ | 1-2x per week (5-10mg/week total) | Noticeable fat loss; improved fasting glucose; better exercise recovery |
| Week 7-12 | 5mg SubQ | 2x per week (10mg/week total max) | Full metabolic optimization; significant insulin sensitivity improvement |
| Week 13-16 | Off-cycle (4 weeks) — continue foundation supplements |

Address the Three Root Causes in order of dependency:

INFLAMMATION

Address first — inflamed systems cannot heal or optimize

INSULIN RESISTANCE

Address second — metabolic dysfunction impairs energy production

ATP SHORTAGE

Address last — mitochondria can only be optimized in a clean environment

Before starting any mitochondrial compound, assess your current mitochondrial health. This checklist identifies whether your mitochondria are healthy enough to respond to peptide stimulation — or whether they need foundational repair first.

If you check three or more of these, your mitochondria are likely compromised. Do NOT start MOTS-c or other mitochondrial peptides until you complete the foundation phase.

If most of these apply to you, your mitochondria are likely ready for peptide stimulation. Proceed with the standard introduction protocol.

While no single blood test measures "mitochondrial health" directly, these markers provide a composite picture:

| Test | What It Reveals | Red Flag Value |
|---|---|---|
| Lactate / Pyruvate Ratio | Aerobic vs. anaerobic metabolism balance | Elevated ratio suggests ETC dysfunction |
| Organic Acids Test (OAT) | Krebs cycle intermediates — shows metabolic bottlenecks | Elevated citrate, succinate, or fumarate |
| CoQ10 Levels (serum) | Electron carrier availability | Below 0.5 mcg/mL |
| hs-CRP | Inflammatory load on mitochondria | Above 3.0 mg/L |
| Fasting Glucose + Insulin | Metabolic flexibility (ability to switch fuel sources) | Glucose above 100 mg/dL or insulin above 8 mIU/L |
| Ferritin | Iron status — iron is essential for ETC cytochromes | Below 30 ng/mL (deficiency) or above 200 (iron overload damages mitochondria) |

If your mitochondrial assessment showed red flags, add these to the basic foundation supplements for an extended 4-6 week preparation phase:

| Supplement | Dose | Mechanism | When to Add |
|---|---|---|---|
| PQQ (Pyrroloquinoline Quinone) | 10-20mg/day | Activates PGC-1alpha — stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis (growth of new mitochondria) | If exercise intolerance or chronic fatigue present |
| NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine) | 600-1200mg/day | Glutathione precursor — primary intracellular antioxidant protecting mitochondrial membranes | If history of oxidative stress, toxin exposure, or mold |
| Alpha Lipoic Acid (R-ALA) | 300-600mg/day | Regenerates both glutathione and CoQ10. Crosses BBB for brain mitochondria support. | If brain fog is a primary complaint |
| Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR) | 500-1000mg/day | Transports fatty acids into mitochondria for beta-oxidation. Supports ATP production from fat. | If metabolic flexibility is impaired (high fasting insulin) |
| NMN (low-dose pre-protocol) | 10mg/day oral | Gentle NAD+ precursor. Begins restoring NAD+ pool before mitochondrial peptides demand it. | Can start with basic foundation supplements |

Every peptide in the DoseCraft system depends on cellular energy to do its work. When mitochondria are dysfunctional, peptides cannot perform — regardless of dose, sourcing quality, or protocol design. This is the dysfunction cascade:

Damaged mitochondria produce insufficient ATP. BPC-157's repair cascades (angiogenesis, collagen synthesis, nerve regeneration) are energy-intensive processes. Without ATP, they stall. You inject BPC-157 and feel nothing — not because the peptide is bad, but because your cells cannot afford to run the repair programs.

MOTS-c activates AMPK — which demands even more mitochondrial capacity. If mitochondria are already maxed out, AMPK activation creates an oxidative burst. The result is paradoxical fatigue: you feel worse after starting the peptide designed to give you energy.

Stressed mitochondria leak reactive oxygen species (ROS) into the cytoplasm, triggering NF-kB activation and inflammatory cytokine release. This inflammation further damages mitochondrial membranes, creating a vicious cycle. Now you are spending money on peptides while your biology actively degrades.

Spend the money on foundation supplements first. Spend the time on 2-6 weeks of preparation. Then — and only then — introduce mitochondrial peptides. This is not optional caution. This is the difference between peptides that transform your biology and peptides that waste your money. **Foundation first. Always.**
