# reconstitution-cheat-sheet

Step-by-step guide to reconstituting, storing, and dosing peptides correctly — so nothing goes to waste.

Peptides are expensive and fragile. One wrong move during reconstitution can destroy an entire vial. This guide ensures you get it right every time.

Gather everything before you begin. Working in a clean, well-lit area reduces contamination risk and mistakes.

Sterile water with 0.9% benzyl alcohol preservative. This preservative prevents bacterial growth after reconstitution, keeping your peptide safe for 25-30 days.

30mL vials are standard. One vial lasts many reconstitutions.Ultra-thin needle minimizes tissue damage and pain. 1mL capacity with 100-unit markings. Use a fresh syringe for every injection — never reuse.

0.5mL syringes work for smaller doses.70% isopropyl alcohol prep pads. Swab every vial top before every needle insertion. Swab injection site before every injection. Non-negotiable hygiene step.

Buy in bulk — you'll use hundreds.Your peptide arrives as a freeze-dried powder (a small puck or cake at the bottom of the vial). This is stable at room temp until reconstituted — but refrigeration extends shelf life.

Check mg amount on label before reconstituting.**NEVER use tap water.** Never use plain sterile water (it has no preservative — bacteria will grow within hours). **Bacteriostatic water ONLY.** The benzyl alcohol preservative is what keeps your reconstituted peptide safe for weeks.

**Before reconstitution:**

Room temperature is fine short-term. Refrigerator (36-46°F / 2-8°C) extends shelf life. Freezer for long-term storage of unreconstituted vials.

**After reconstitution:**

Refrigerator ONLY — **36-46°F (2-8°C)**. Never freeze after reconstituting. Never leave at room temperature. Use within 25-30 days.

Follow these steps exactly. Rushing or skipping steps destroys peptides and wastes money.

Wipe the rubber stopper of both the BAC water vial and the peptide vial. Let air dry for 5 seconds. This prevents contamination from manufacturing residue and handling.

Insert needle into BAC water vial. Invert the vial. Slowly draw 1mL (100 units on an insulin syringe). Remove air bubbles by tapping the syringe and pushing them out.

Pierce the rubber stopper at a slight angle. Position the needle tip so it touches the **inside wall of the glass vial**, NOT pointing directly at the powder.

Push the plunger **extremely slowly**. The water should trickle down the inside wall of the vial onto the powder. **DO NOT squirt directly onto the powder.** Direct force breaks peptide bonds.

Withdraw the needle carefully. Set the vial down gently. Resist every urge to shake it. Shaking denatures the peptide chains and renders them useless.

Roll the vial between your palms with light pressure. Think: **"treat it like a newborn."** Gentle rotation helps the powder dissolve without damaging the fragile peptide structure.

Set the vial upright in the refrigerator. Most peptides dissolve within 5-10 minutes. Some may take longer. The solution should be completely clear — no floating particles or cloudiness.

Once dissolved, the vial stays in the fridge at 36-46°F. Every minute at room temperature reduces potency. Mark the vial with the reconstitution date. Use within 25-30 days.

**Peptides are fragments — they are fragile.** They are short chains of amino acids held together by delicate bonds. Aggressive handling (shaking, squirting, heat exposure) physically destroys these bonds. A degraded peptide is a wasted peptide.

Once reconstituted, you need to know exactly how much to draw. This is simple division — but getting it wrong means underdosing or overdosing.

Peptide Amount (mcg) ÷ BAC Water (mL) = Concentration (mcg/mL)

Then: **Desired Dose (mcg) ÷ Concentration (mcg/mL) = Volume to Inject (mL)**

| Vial Size | BAC Water | Concentration | For 500mcg Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5mg (5000mcg) | 1mL | 5000 mcg/mL | 0.1mL (10 units) |
| 10mg (10,000mcg) | 2mL | 5000 mcg/mL | 0.1mL (10 units) |
| 5mg (5000mcg) | 2mL | 2500 mcg/mL | 0.2mL (20 units) |

A standard **100-unit insulin syringe** holds 1mL total. Each small tick mark = **1 unit = 0.01mL**.

With a 0.5mL syringe, each tick = 1 unit = 0.01mL, but max capacity is 50 units (0.5mL). These are more precise for small doses.

Proper storage is the difference between a peptide that works and expensive water.

| State | Storage Method | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-reconstitution (powder) | Freezer or refrigerator | Months to years |
| Post-reconstitution (liquid) | Refrigerator only (36-46°F) | 25-30 days max |
| Freeze after reconstitution? | NEVER | Destroys peptide structure |
| Room temperature (post-recon)? | NEVER | Rapid degradation + bacterial growth |

Shaking creates micro-bubbles and shear forces that break peptide bonds. Always swirl gently. Never shake, tap, or flick.

Tap water introduces bacteria, minerals, and chlorine. Sterile water (without benzyl alcohol) has no preservative — bacteria colonize within hours. BAC water only.

Reconstituted peptides degrade rapidly above 46°F. Every hour at room temp reduces potency. Get it back in the fridge immediately after drawing your dose.

Reused needles are dull (causing tissue damage), contaminated (introducing bacteria into the vial), and dangerous. One syringe per injection. No exceptions.

Injecting the same spot repeatedly causes lipodystrophy (fat tissue damage), scarring, and reduced absorption. Rotate: abdomen (left/right), thighs, upper arms.
