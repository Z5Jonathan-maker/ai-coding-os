# bloodwork-checklist

The markers every peptide user needs tested — *before* your first injection.

Every marker in this checklist is mapped to one of the 3 Root Causes so you know exactly which biological failure to fix first.

73% of peptide users never check baseline markers before starting a protocol. They spend hundreds on compounds — and have zero idea if they're helping or hurting.

How do you know your BPC-157 is reducing inflammation if you never measured CRP before starting? How do you know Ipamorelin is boosting GH output if you never measured IGF-1 at baseline?

Without pre-protocol labs, every compound is a gamble. You might feel "better" — but you have no data to separate placebo from real biological change.

The **3 Root Causes Framework** identifies three biological failures behind chronic disease and aging: Inflammation, Insulin Resistance, and ATP Shortage.

Your bloodwork tells you which one is dominant — and that determines which peptides will actually move the needle for *your* biology.

**GH secretagogues + elevated insulin = making things WORSE.** Growth hormone naturally antagonizes insulin. If your fasting insulin is above 8 mIU/L, compounds like Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, and Tesamorelin can push you deeper into metabolic dysfunction. This is exactly the kind of disaster that one simple blood test prevents.

These five markers form the absolute minimum. Do not start any peptide protocol without these results in hand.

The single most important metabolic marker. Tells you if your cells are deaf to insulin's signal. Elevated fasting insulin means GH secretagogues are contraindicated until this is fixed. Standard lab ranges go up to 24 — that's metabolic disaster territory. Optimal is below 5.

Your systemic fire alarm. CRP rises in response to IL-6, the master inflammatory cytokine. Above 3.0 means your body is running a chronic, body-wide blaze — and anti-inflammatory peptides should be your first priority, not GH compounds.

The downstream marker of growth hormone output. Without a baseline IGF-1, you have zero way to know if your GH secretagogue is actually doing anything. This is the before-and-after proof marker for every Ipamorelin, CJC-1295, or Tesamorelin protocol.

Red cells, white cells, platelets, hemoglobin, hematocrit. Screens for anemia, infection, immune dysfunction, and blood cancers. BPC-157 and TB-500 can influence angiogenesis — baseline blood counts are essential safety data.

Covers liver enzymes (AST, ALT), kidney function (BUN, creatinine), electrolytes, and fasting glucose. Peptides are processed by the liver and kidneys — you need to know these organs are functioning properly before introducing exogenous compounds.

Tier 1 costs $50–100 at discount labs (Walk-In Lab, Ulta Lab Tests). Tier 2 adds $100–200. Tier 3 (especially OAT) can run $200–400. **Start with Tier 1. Always.** Add Tier 2 when budget allows. Tier 3 is for targeted investigation when something isn't adding up.

Baseline is everything. Without it, follow-up labs are meaningless numbers.

Get Tier 1 labs **before your first injection**. This is your baseline. Without it, nothing that follows has context.

Retest key markers at **8–12 weeks** into protocol. This is where you see real biological change — or lack thereof.

Ongoing monitoring every **6 months**. Catches drift, seasonal variation, and long-term trends.
