cognition, repair, immunity, performance.

THE RULES
7 Non-Negotiable Beginner Rules
Protocols don't fail because the peptides don't work. They fail because people skip the basics. Tattoo
these rules on your brain.
1 Start With ONE Compound
Don't stack everything on Day 1. Start with BPC-157 alone for 4-6 weeks. Establish your baseline
response. Then add compounds one at a time, 2-3 weeks apart, so you know exactly what each
one does for your body.
2 Injectable Only — Oral Peptides Don't Work
Peptides are amino acid chains. Your digestive system's entire job is to destroy amino acid
chains. Oral capsules are broken down by proteolytic enzymes before reaching systemic
circulation. If it's not injected SubQ, you're wasting your money.
3 Get Baseline Bloodwork BEFORE Starting
You can't measure improvement without a starting point. Before your first injection, get: CRP
(inflammation), IGF-1 (growth factor status), Homocysteine (cardiovascular risk), fasting
glucose, and a full metabolic panel.
4 Track Everything
"A short pencil is better than a long memory." Log every injection — compound, dose, site, time.
Track subjective markers: energy, sleep, pain, mood. Without data, you're flying blind. With data,
you're optimizing.
5 Don't Stop When You Feel Better
Pain resolution occurs at 40-60% tissue repair. The remaining remodeling — collagen alignment,
cross-link strengthening, vascular network maturation — takes weeks after symptoms resolve.
Complete the full protocol. Every time.
