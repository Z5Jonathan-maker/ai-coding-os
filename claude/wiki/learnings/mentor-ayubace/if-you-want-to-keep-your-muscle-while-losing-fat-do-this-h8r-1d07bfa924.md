# If you want to KEEP your muscle while losing fat.. Do This

If you're on a cut and you're training and you're still finding that you're losing a bit of strength, make sure you're properly hydrated. Not just through drinking electrolytes and drinking water, but make sure you're putting enough salt into your diet. People seem to think salt is bad. Salt makes you hold water. Yeah, it does. Makes you hold water within the muscle. You want to have a hydrated muscle because a hydrated muscle is a functioning muscle. Secondly, hammering the cardio could be putting yourself in a situation where you're losing those strength gain. So, you might be better off dropping the cardio down and increasing your steps. When you're cutting, you're always on this like fine balancing act of pushing and not pushing so much that you're almost going to be counterproductive.
