Most peptide content has no evidence grading. Everything is presented equally — a randomized controlled trial carries the same visual weight as one person's forum post. DoseCraft's Three Evidence Lanes system lets you see the strength behind every claim.

Peptide science sits at a unique intersection: rigorous clinical research exists for some compounds, while others rely entirely on practitioner observation and animal data. A binary "proven/unproven" label loses critical nuance. Three lanes let you calibrate your confidence — and your caution — to the actual strength of the evidence.

Clinical-tagged claims represent the **highest confidence level**. These compounds have been tested in controlled human settings with measurable outcomes. Dosing ranges from this lane are the most reliable. However, "clinical" does not mean "guaranteed" — individual variation and compound sourcing affect real-world results.

Expert-tagged claims carry **strong practical credibility** but lack controlled conditions of clinical trials. Practitioner bias and placebo response are not controlled for. These protocols work in practice — but the "why" may not be fully confirmed.

Many of the most promising peptide compounds began their journey in this lane. **BPC-157's initial data was almost entirely animal-based** before practitioners adopted it clinically. Experimental data provides the mechanistic rationale and early safety signals that guide clinical practice.

Experimental-tagged claims should be approached with appropriate caution. Rodent metabolisms differ from human. Cell cultures lack systemic complexity. N=1 reports are subject to placebo, confirmation bias, and uncontrolled variables. Use this data to inform hypotheses, not to set dosing protocols.

| Lane | Supporting Evidence |

| Experimental | Rodent studies show accelerated wound healing timelines of 40-60%. VEGF upregulation and NO pathway activation demonstrated in vitro. Cytoprotective effects confirmed across 100+ animal studies. |

**Confidence:** High — supported across all three lanes with convergent evidence from clinical, practitioner, and experimental data.
