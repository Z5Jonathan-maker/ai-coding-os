# 20-wolverine-protocol

The definitive injury-healing stack. Three compounds. Site-specific + systemic. For acute injuries, chronic pain, and post-surgical recovery — heal like it's your superpower.

A signal caller. A foreman. A finisher. The three-phase injury repair system that practitioners use for everything from torn tendons to post-surgical recovery.

Each compound plays a distinct role in the healing cascade. BPC-157 signals the repair. TB-500 regenerates the tissue. GHK-Cu finishes the job with quality control. Together they compress weeks of healing into days.

The master repair signal. BPC-157 tells the body: "Fix THIS. Now." It orchestrates VEGF-mediated angiogenesis, promotes collagen synthesis via TGF-beta 1, and modulates the inflammatory response at the injury site.

| Dose | Route | Site | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1mg/day | SubQ injection | AT THE INJURY SITE | 14 days minimum |

The tissue regeneration specialist. TB-500 regenerates muscle fibers, reorganizes scar tissue, improves flexibility dramatically, and heals cardiac tissue. Works systemically — it finds the damage wherever it is.

| Dose | Route | Site | Schedule |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5mg | SubQ injection | Belly fat (systemic) | Every 5 days |

Quality control. GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring tripeptide that binds to copper. It cleans up all oxidative stress from the injury, regulates tissue remodeling, and ensures healing is complete and properly finished — not just patched.

| Dose | Route | Site | Schedule |
|---|---|---|---|
| 300mcg/day | SubQ injection | Belly fat (systemic) | Daily for 4-6 weeks |

This is the most important rule. BPC-157 is site-specific — its VEGF-mediated repair cascade is triggered where you inject it. Elbow injury? Inject near the elbow. Knee injury? Near the knee. Knuckle, ankle, shoulder — wherever the damage is.

Use a 29-gauge insulin needle. Inject SubQ within a few inches of the injury site. Avoid major nerves (e.g., ulnar nerve at the elbow).

TB-500 and GHK-Cu work systemically. They don't need to be injected at the injury site. Grab a pinch of belly fat, fire it in. These compounds circulate through the bloodstream and find the damage on their own. You CAN mix TB-500 and GHK-Cu in the same syringe for convenience — but note they have different daily schedules (TB-500 every 5 days, GHK-Cu daily).

Pain reduction does not mean healing is complete. Tissue remodeling continues for weeks after pain subsides. **Complete the full 4-6 week protocol** regardless of when symptoms resolve. Stopping early risks incomplete healing, scar tissue formation, and re-injury. The most common mistake is feeling better at week 2 and quitting.

The Wolverine Protocol is an amplifier, not invincibility juice. You still need smart rehabilitation and movement. The peptides dramatically accelerate healing, but the body needs mechanical loading and proper movement patterns to rebuild functional tissue — not just fill in gaps. Work with a physiotherapist or follow a structured rehab program alongside the protocol.

**The synergy:** BPC-157 builds the infrastructure (blood vessels, collagen scaffolding, inflammatory control) while TB-500 fills that infrastructure with functional, flexible tissue. BPC-157 opens the construction site; TB-500 builds the structure. Without BPC-157's vascular groundwork, TB-500 cannot deliver materials efficiently. Without TB-500's structural capacity, BPC-157's scaffolding remains empty.

For acute injuries, higher BPC-157 dosing and more frequent TB-500 loading capitalize on the initial inflammatory response window. For chronic injuries, steady lower dosing over longer periods remodels established scar tissue.

BPC-157's repair mechanism is **paracrine** (locally acting), not endocrine (systemically acting). When injected subcutaneously near the injury, BPC-157 creates a **high local concentration gradient** that triggers VEGF-mediated angiogenesis, fibroblast recruitment, and collagen deposition specifically at the target tissue. Injecting into abdominal fat distributes the peptide systemically, diluting the local concentration by orders of magnitude.

**The rule:** Inject BPC-157 within **1–3 inches of the injury**. SubQ, not intramuscular. Use a 29-gauge insulin needle. The goal is to flood the surrounding tissue with repair signals. If you cannot access the injury site directly (e.g., deep spinal injury), inject at the closest accessible subcutaneous point. TB-500 and GHK-Cu do NOT require site-specific injection — they are systemically active.
