## Bloodwork Cheat Sheet

Optimal ranges and action triggers for the biomarkers that matter most in peptide protocol monitoring.

Biomarker / Optimal / Concern

If Abnormal → Consider

hs-CRP (Inflammation)

Optimal: **<1.0 mg/L** | Concern: **>3.0 mg/L**

If elevated → **BPC-157, KPV, Thymosin Alpha-1**. Address root inflammation before adding metabolic compounds.

Fasting Insulin (Metabolic)

Optimal: **2-5 mIU/L** | Concern: **>8 mIU/L**

If elevated → **MOTS-c, Retatrutide**. Do NOT add GH secretagogues until insulin normalizes.

IGF-1 (Growth)

Optimal: **150-250 ng/mL** | Concern: **>350 ng/mL**

If low → consider Ipamorelin/CJC-1295. If elevated → **reduce or stop GH secretagogues**. Cancer risk consideration.

HbA1c (Metabolic)

Optimal: **<5.3%** | Concern: **>5.7%**

If elevated → **MOTS-c, Retatrutide, 5-Amino-1MQ**. Prioritize insulin sensitization over all other goals.

Homocysteine (Cardiovascular)

Optimal: **<7 umol/L** | Concern: **>12 umol/L**

If elevated → methylated B-vitamins first (B12, folate, B6). Address before peptide protocols. Cardiovascular risk marker.

Total Testosterone (Hormonal)

Optimal M: **600-900 ng/dL** | Concern: **<400**

If low → **Gonadorelin, Enclomiphene, or TRT + HCG**. Optimize before adding performance peptides.

Free Testosterone (Hormonal)

Optimal M: **15-25 pg/mL** | Concern: **<10**

If low despite normal Total T → investigate SHBG. Boron supplementation may help. Functional testosterone is what matters.

Estradiol (E2) (Hormonal)

Optimal M: **20-35 pg/mL** | Concern: **>50**

If elevated on TRT → adjust TRT dose or add AI (low-dose anastrozole). Do NOT crash E2 — joint pain and mood consequences.

TSH (Thyroid)

Optimal: **1.0-2.0 mIU/L** | Concern: **>4.0**

If elevated → check Free T3/T4 and antibodies. Thyroid function affects all metabolic peptides. Optimize before MOTS-c.

Free T3 (Thyroid)

Optimal: **3.0-4.0 pg/mL** | Concern: **<2.5**

If low → evaluate conversion (T4 to T3). Selenium, zinc, and adequate calories support conversion. Active thyroid hormone.

Vitamin D (25-OH) (Foundational)

Optimal: **60-80 ng/mL** | Concern: **<30**

If low → supplement D3 + K2. Foundation for immune and hormonal function. Suboptimal levels blunt Thymosin Alpha-1 response.

Hematocrit (Safety)

Optimal: **42-50%** | Concern: **>54%**

If elevated on TRT/GH → **donate blood or reduce dose**. Stroke and clotting risk. Monitor every 3 months on TRT.

Fasting Glucose (Metabolic)

Optimal: **75-85 mg/dL** | Concern: **>100**

If elevated → **MOTS-c, Retatrutide**. Combined with fasting insulin, this reveals true metabolic health status.

Ranges reflect practitioner-validated optimal targets, not conventional lab reference ranges. Always interpret bloodwork with your healthcare provider. See Chapter 28 for the complete bloodwork monitoring guide.

#### Insulin Syringe Reading Guide

**Read at the plunger edge closest to the needle.** Each small tick = 1 unit (0.01 mL). Draw to the number of units shown in the lookup tables above.

#### Universal Reconstitution Formula

Units to Draw = (Desired Dose ÷ Concentration) × 100

Example: Want 500mcg from a 5mg vial reconstituted with 2mL BAC water?

Concentration = 5mg ÷ 2mL = 2.5mg/mL = 2,500mcg/mL

**Units = (500 ÷ 2,500) × 100 = 20 units**

## Injection Site Map

Proper site selection and rotation prevent scar tissue buildup, reduce injection pain, and optimize absorption. See Chapter 25 for the complete injection technique guide.

A

#### Abdominal SubQ — Primary Site

2+ inches from navel, pinch belly fat, 45-degree angle. 29-gauge insulin needle. Used for **most peptides:** BPC-157, MOTS-c, Thymosin Alpha-1, KPV, Epitalon, Ipamorelin/CJC-1295.

Rotate between 4 quadrants: upper-left, upper-right, lower-left, lower-right.

B

#### Deltoid SubQ — Alternate Site

Outer upper arm, pinch fat fold over deltoid. Good for leaner individuals with limited abdominal fat. Same needle gauge.

Alternate between left and right deltoid.

C

#### Vastus Lateralis IM — Alternate IM

Outer mid-thigh. 25-gauge, 1-inch needle at 90 degrees. Option for IM compounds if glute access is limited. Used for larger volumes.

D

#### Ventrogluteal IM — For Loading Doses

Upper-outer glute quadrant. 25-gauge, 1-1.5 inch needle at 90 degrees. **Required for:** TB-500 loading doses (5mg), Cerebrolysin, larger IM volumes.

Avoid sciatic nerve zone (inner/lower quadrant). If unsure, use vastus lateralis instead.

↻
#### Rotate Sites Daily

Never inject the same spot two days in a row. Use a clockwise rotation pattern through your preferred sites. Log injection locations in your protocol journal.

Sterile Technique Required
Always swab injection site and vial top with alcohol prep pad. Use a fresh needle for every injection. Never re-cap needles by hand. Dispose of sharps in a proper sharps container. If redness, swelling, or warmth develops at an injection site, stop injecting that site and monitor for infection.
