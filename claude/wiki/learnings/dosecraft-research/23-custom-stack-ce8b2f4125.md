# 23-custom-stack

Not everyone has the same goals or the same budget. Use this decision framework to build a personalized protocol from the ground up — starting with one compound and scaling based on your needs and resources.

Your goal. Your budget. Your timeline. One framework to build any stack — from a $300/month starter to a $1,000/month powerhouse.

Every custom stack begins with BPC-157. Run it solo for 2 weeks minimum. It addresses the most universal dysfunction (systemic inflammation), has the strongest safety profile, and establishes your baseline response to peptide therapy. If BPC-157 doesn't produce noticeable effects, you likely have a sourcing quality problem — not a protocol problem.

Your second compound should address your dominant root cause failure — not the compound that has the most online hype. Use bloodwork to identify whether inflammation, insulin resistance, or ATP shortage is your primary bottleneck. Then select accordingly.

A short pencil is way better than a long memory. Track pain levels, sleep quality, energy, mood, body composition, and exercise performance weekly. Without data, you're guessing. With data, you're engineering.

Never buy pre-mixed blends. They remove your ability to dose independently, isolate effects, and adjust based on response. Individual peptides cost the same or less and give you complete control. Blends steal your money.

The complete stack is not the starting point — it's the destination. Start with the Trilogy ($300/mo). Graduate to the Top 4 ($500/mo). Scale toward the Full Longevity Stack ($1,000/mo) as your experience, bloodwork data, and budget allow. Patience is a protocol design principle.

Refer to the Decision Framework on the previous page. Pick ONE primary goal: Recovery, Performance, Longevity, or Cognitive. This determines your core compound. Do not try to solve everything at once.

Before spending a dollar on peptides, get the baseline panel from Chapter 28: CRP, fasting insulin, HbA1c, IGF-1, DHEA-S, homocysteine. This identifies your dominant root cause and gives you data to measure progress against. Cost: $100–200 via direct-to-consumer labs.

Run BPC-157 alone for 14 days. This establishes your personal response baseline, confirms your vendor quality, and begins addressing systemic inflammation. Track subjective markers weekly (see Chapter 28 journal template).

After 2 weeks of BPC-157, add one compound targeting your dominant root cause. Run this duo for 4 weeks. If your primary issue is metabolic (high insulin), add MOTS-c. If energy/ATP, add Methylene Blue. If immune, add Thymosin Alpha-1. One new compound at a time.

Before adding any compound, cross-reference the Drug Interaction Matrix in Chapter 31. Key conflicts: Methylene Blue + SSRIs (fatal), GH secretagogues + high insulin (worsens IR), GLP-1 agonists + other GLP-1 agonists (never combine). If you take prescription medications, consult the full interaction table.

Get 6-month bloodwork. Compare to baseline. If inflammation is down and insulin improved, your foundation is working — consider adding a third compound. If markers have not moved, investigate: vendor quality, dosing accuracy, lifestyle factors. Never add complexity to a stack that is not producing measurable results.
