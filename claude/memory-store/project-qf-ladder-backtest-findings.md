**Results (1 micro/entry, lock50 mgmt):**
- Live replay of TODAY's tape with his EXACT cycle (limit@QF, stop above wick, re-enter) = +12.6R, every leg green — his method works beautifully on a range day.
- Fixed initial-stop buffer sweep (open VWAP-dir setup): wider buffer buys win% (43→48%) but net barely moves (~$36k) and DD climbs. Buffer is NOT the lever.
- Raw all-day ladder (fade every tap both ways): ~$910/yr/micro, ~breakeven-ish, DD $2,389, 6 entries/day. Fades into trends = bleed.
- Swing-extreme filter (only fade fresh local highs/lows): WORSE, goes negative — fading into momentum.
- Trend-bias filter (SMA30 slope: long-only uptrend / short-only downtrend, don't fight): ~$1,689/yr/micro, lower DD $2,173 — ~2x raw, the best mechanical lever. Matches his chart (bought support dips up, shorted only at the top).

**Conclusion (the important one):** even tuned, the mechanical ladder is only ~$1.7k/yr/micro — a fraction of what he does live scaling accounts. His edge is NOT in any mechanical rule inferable from price alone; it's the discretionary **SELECTION** — the robot takes ~6 taps/day, he takes a handful and they're the right ones. Same lesson as [[feedback-backtest-measures-robot-not-trader]] and [[feedback-defer-to-trading-instinct]]. See [[feedback-qf-two-phase-premove]] for the confirmed entry/management spec.

**SKIP RULES = TWO-REGIME MODEL (answered 2026-06-15 via his fully-annotated YM chart):** he runs the same QF ladder in two regimes and knows which from MARKET STRUCTURE:
1. **Range -> ping-pong** (his label "this entire range is ping pong strategy"): while price oscillates between QFs, fade rung-to-rung (short upper QF, buy lower QF).
2. **Breakout -> QF ROLE-REVERSAL** (his labels "Break Out & HL", "breakout & HL - testing QF as support and buy entry"): when a QF breaks and price makes a Higher Low, that QF flips resistance->support; BUY the retest that holds and ride it (the big blue up-arrow). Mirror for breakdown+LH.
**The skip rule:** NEVER fade a QF the bias is breaking through. HL/HH=bullish -> long QF-as-support only; LH/LL=bearish -> short QF-as-resistance only; range -> ping-pong both ways. This kills the raw-ladder trend bleed.

**COIL = LEVERAGE PLAY (2026-06-15, Jonathan: "what makes coils work is entering MANY contracts, heavily leveraged"):** per-micro coil $ is small BY DESIGN; the strategy is to stack many contracts on a high-prob coil. backtest/coil_leverage.py (corrected re-entry/stack sequences, $2k compound): YM 80%-selection -> ~$1M (10% risk, 46%DD) up to $1.08M (30%, 24%DD); NQ -> $343k (10%, 70%DD); ES -> $62k (10%, 81%DD). Thesis confirmed: leveraged, the coil is a rocket. BUT: (1) half-Kelly (20-25%) RUINS NQ/ES even at 80% win — stacked units stop together (correlated) + small-sample fat tails Kelly doesn't price; empirical safe = 5-10%. (2) HIGH-VARIANCE: 24-81% drawdowns even sized right (vs breakout book's 10-21%). FORMULA codified in service/coil_sizing.py (bot-reproducible): risk_frac = min(8%, 0.5*kelly); base = floor(equity*risk_frac/(stop_pts*point_value)); +base each fresh QF bounce up to 8 stacks; exit all at range edge/stop. MAX_FRAC capped 8% (empirical, NOT theoretical Kelly). Higher win rate -> more contracts (leverage earned). Coil stays a high-variance booster, separate from the smooth breakout compounder.

**FILL REALISM — IS THE TIGHT STOP BACKTESTABLE? (2026-06-16, Jonathan: "no way to backtest that?"):** YES, partially — built backtest/fill_realism.py (clones the locked breakout ride + coil exit with a `slip` knob + gap-through recording; ping_pong/breakout_retest UNTOUCHED; slip=0 reproduces locked numbers exactly). Two measurements off the 1m tape (no tick data needed):
- **Slippage sensitivity** (re-run with N pts adverse fill on every entry/stop/target). **BREAKOUT = slippage-PROOF:** 0pt +3.39R/$21.9k/yr → 3pt +2.34R/$17.3k → 5pt +1.80R/$14.4k → 8pt still +1.16R. Winners are +250pt so a few pts of slip is noise. The 15-35pt breakout stop is a non-issue; deploy with confidence. **COIL = slippage-FRAGILE (opposite):** 79% win @0 → 74 @1 → 68 @2 → 65 @3 → 57 @5; net 970pt → 120pt by 5slip. A tight-target scalp has thin per-trade edge, so each pt of slip eats ~5% win rate.
- **Gap-through** (how far the triggering bar travels past the stop, + gap-opens): breakout 3592 stop-outs, only **4 (0%) gapped open past the stop** — 99.9% trade through → stop-market fills ≈ at the level. Coil only 10 stop-outs (79% win), median excursion 2pt, max 7pt, **0 gap-opens**. So the 15pt stop itself HOLDS — price doesn't blow through it; the coil danger is NOT catastrophic gap-through, it's the accumulation of small per-fill slip eroding win rate.
- **SAFETY FIX (the leverage double-count):** coil sizes its STACK off win rate (higher W → more Kelly → heavier load), so an optimistic W inflates BOTH edge and leverage. Added `sizing_win_rate(backtest_win, fills_validated=False)` to service/coil_sizing.py — until live fills are proven, derate W by WIN_DERATE_PER_PT(0.05)*ASSUMED_SLIP_PTS(2.0) → size off ~69%, not 79%. Strengthens (never weakens) the rail. +5 tests (tests/test_coil_sizing.py), 135 green. **What's STILL not backtestable:** queue position + actual fill-at-the-touch in real microstructure (needs L1/L2 tick) — that remains the paper-test gate, but it's now bounded, not a black box.
