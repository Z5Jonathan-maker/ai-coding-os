# 35-recovery-protocols

From torn tendons to traumatic brain injuries — matching the right peptide to the right tissue type is the difference between months and weeks of recovery.

Signal Caller — 1mg/day at injury site for 14+ days

Foreman — 5mg every 5 days, belly fat (systemic)

Finisher — 300mcg/day, belly fat (systemic)

Do NOT stop when pain stops. Complete the full 4-6 week protocol. Pain resolution precedes tissue remodeling — stopping early means structurally incomplete healing. "This is an amplifier, not invincibility juice."

| Gut (leaky gut, IBD, ulcers) | BPC-157 500mcg/day | KPV (anti-inflammatory) | SubQ in belly fat (systemic) | 4-8 weeks |

Peptides amplify your body's repair capacity, but they cannot create tissue from nothing. Proper nutrition provides the raw materials that peptides direct into repair.

Peptides are amplifiers, not replacements for proper rehabilitation. The combination is greater than either alone.

The first 72 hours after injury determine the trajectory of healing. Starting peptides immediately — within hours, not days — provides the greatest advantage. This protocol covers the critical window between injury and standard medical care.

| Timeframe | Action | Compounds | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-6 hours | First injection as soon as possible | BPC-157 1mg SubQ near injury site | Immediately activates VEGF, initiates angiogenesis, begins anti-inflammatory signaling cascade. The sooner BPC-157 reaches damaged tissue, the better the outcome. |
| 6-24 hours | Add systemic support | BPC-157 1mg (2nd dose) + KPV (per protocol) | Second BPC-157 dose reinforces repair signaling. KPV begins suppressing NF-kB to prevent excessive inflammatory damage to surrounding healthy tissue. |
| 24-48 hours | Begin full Wolverine Protocol | BPC-157 1mg/day + TB-500 5mg (first loading dose) | TB-500 begins systemic tissue regeneration. Loading dose ensures rapid tissue saturation. BPC-157 continues directing repair. |
| 48-72 hours | Add tissue finishing compound | Add GHK-Cu 300mcg/day | GHK-Cu begins oxidative debris cleanup and prevents scar tissue formation. Early introduction improves long-term tissue quality. |

Suspected fractures, dislocations, spinal injuries, deep lacerations, or any injury requiring surgical evaluation should receive emergency medical care FIRST. Peptides support healing — they do not replace splinting, imaging, surgical repair, or emergency medicine. Start peptide protocols after initial medical stabilization.

Chronic injuries (tendinopathy, degenerative joint disease, chronic ligament laxity) require a different approach than acute injuries. The tissue is not actively inflamed — it is degenerating, fibrotic, or poorly vascularized. The goal is to restart the healing cascade in tissue that has stopped trying to heal.

| Phase | Duration | Compounds | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1: Re-ignite | Weeks 1-4 | BPC-157 1mg/day (at site) + TB-500 5mg every 5 days | BPC-157 restarts angiogenesis in avascular tissue. TB-500 breaks down existing scar tissue and promotes actin reorganization. Expect initial soreness — this is the repair cascade reactivating. |
| Phase 2: Rebuild | Weeks 5-8 | Continue BPC + TB + add GHK-Cu 300mcg/day + eccentric loading exercises | Full Wolverine Protocol running. GHK-Cu improves collagen quality. Eccentric loading (controlled lengthening under load) stimulates tendon remodeling that peptides cannot trigger alone. |
| Phase 3: Strengthen | Weeks 9-12 | BPC-157 500mcg/day (reduced) + GHK-Cu + progressive loading | Taper TB-500. Transition to progressive resistance training. BPC-157 continues supporting new tissue. GHK-Cu ensures quality collagen deposition. |
| Phase 4: Maintenance | Ongoing | BPC-157 500mcg-1mg/day as needed | Low-dose maintenance for chronically vulnerable tissues. Some clinical experts recommend indefinite low-dose BPC-157 for athletes with recurrent tendon issues. |

Surgical recovery is the ideal use case for peptide support — you know the exact tissue damaged, the timeline of injury, and you have medical oversight. Coordinate with your surgeon on timing.

| Surgical Phase | Timing | Compounds | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-surgical prep | 2-4 weeks before surgery | BPC-157 500mcg/day + GHK-Cu 200mcg/day | Primes tissue for accelerated healing. Upregulates growth factor receptors. Improves local blood supply to surgical area. Discontinue 48 hours before surgery (precautionary — no known surgical interaction, but standard practice). |
| Post-op Day 1-3 | Resume ASAP after surgeon clearance | BPC-157 1mg/day (SubQ near surgical site, not in wound) + KPV | Inject 2-3 inches from incision site — NOT into the wound. KPV controls post-surgical inflammatory response. Coordinate timing with surgeon. |
| Post-op Week 1-4 | Full Wolverine Protocol | BPC-157 1mg/day + TB-500 5mg/5 days + GHK-Cu 300mcg/day | Maximum regenerative support. TB-500 prevents excessive fibrosis (scar tissue). GHK-Cu ensures high-quality tissue remodeling. BPC-157 orchestrates the entire process. |
| Post-op Week 5-8 | Transition to maintenance | BPC-157 500mcg/day + GHK-Cu 200mcg/day | Taper TB-500. Continue BPC-157 and GHK-Cu for tissue finishing. Begin physical therapy if cleared. |

Loading doses saturate tissues and establish repair cascades. Maintenance doses sustain the regenerative environment. Skipping the loading phase reduces total efficacy by an estimated 30-40%.
