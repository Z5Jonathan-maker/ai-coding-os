# 25-injection-technique

SubQ is the primary route. Site-specific is the secret weapon. Here is every detail you need to inject safely and effectively.

Most peptide failures have nothing to do with the compound — they come from poor administration. This chapter makes that impossible.

Not all delivery methods are created equal. The route determines bioavailability, speed of action, and whether the compound survives long enough to do anything.

| Route | Bioavailability | Use Case | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| SubQ Injection | ~100% | ALL peptides — primary route | GOLD STANDARD |
| Site-Specific SubQ | ~100% | BPC-157 for injuries — inject near the injury | INJURY PROTOCOL |
| IM Injection | ~100% | Not emphasized — SubQ is sufficient | UNNECESSARY |
| Nasal | Variable | Semax (designed for nasal delivery) | EXCEPTION ONLY |
| Topical | Low-Moderate | KPV, GHK-Cu for skin conditions | EXCEPTION ONLY |
| Oral | <2% | Destroyed by stomach acid and liver | NEVER FOR PEPTIDES |

This is the only needle you need. Available on Amazon, pharmacies, and medical supply stores.

SPECIFICATIONS

WHY 29-GAUGE

Expert 29-gauge is the standard across leading practitioner protocols. No need for separate drawing and injection needles at this gauge.

For systemic compounds: lower abdomen (2 inches from navel), alternating left and right. Avoid scar tissue, bruises, and the navel itself.

Wipe the skin with an alcohol swab in a circular motion outward. Let it air dry for 10 seconds. Do not blow on it.

Use your non-dominant hand to pinch and lift a fold of skin and subcutaneous fat. Hold it firmly throughout the injection. This creates a target and prevents hitting muscle.

With a good fat pinch: 90 degrees (straight in). Leaner individuals: 45 degrees. The needle should enter the subcutaneous fat layer, not muscle. With a 29-gauge 1/2-inch needle, you almost cannot go too deep.

**Never rush.** Depress the plunger slowly and steadily over 5-10 seconds. Fast injection causes tissue irritation, pain, and sub-optimal absorption. Slow and smooth wins.

Pull the needle out at the same angle. Apply light pressure with an alcohol swab if needed. No rubbing. Dispose of the syringe in a sharps container immediately.

BPC-157 is the one compound where injection location dramatically affects outcomes. For injuries, you inject subcutaneously **as close to the injury as anatomically practical**. The peptide concentrates locally and orchestrates repair at the site.

| Injury Location | Injection Site | Technique Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Knee (ACL, meniscus, patellar) | SubQ around the knee — medial or lateral | Pinch whatever skin/fat available; lean tissue is fine |
| Elbow (tennis elbow, UCL) | SubQ near elbow — lateral or medial epicondyle | Avoid the ulnar nerve (funny bone area) |
| Shoulder (rotator cuff, labrum) | SubQ at the deltoid/shoulder capsule area | Plenty of tissue to pinch here |
| Ankle / Achilles | SubQ near the lateral or posterior ankle | Thin tissue — use 45-degree angle |
| Wrist / Hand / Knuckles | SubQ on the dorsal (back) side of the hand | Very thin tissue — gentle, shallow insertion |
| Spine / Lower back | SubQ along paraspinal muscles | Partner may be needed for access |
| Gut (leaky gut, IBS) | SubQ into abdominal fat (systemic) | Belly fat injection — standard technique |

Everything that is NOT BPC-157 for injuries gets injected into abdominal subcutaneous fat. These compounds work systemically — they need to enter general circulation, not concentrate at a specific site.

Systemic tissue repair. Belly fat injection. Works throughout the entire body — do NOT inject at injury site.

Quality control and finishing. Belly fat injection. Systemic anti-aging and oxidative cleanup.

Immune and metabolic. Belly fat. "Grab a pinch, fire it in."

Rotating injection sites prevents lipodystrophy (hardened tissue), bruising, and localized irritation. Follow a clockwise pattern around the navel, staying at least 2 inches away from the belly button.

| Day | Position | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Upper Left | 2 inches left of navel, 2 inches above |
| Tuesday | Upper Right | 2 inches right of navel, 2 inches above |
| Wednesday | Lower Left | 2 inches left of navel, 2 inches below |
| Thursday | Lower Right | 2 inches right of navel, 2 inches below |
| Friday | Far Left | 4 inches left of navel, at navel height |
| Saturday | Far Right | 4 inches right of navel, at navel height |
| Sunday | Below Navel | 3 inches directly below navel |

Speed of injection directly impacts absorption quality and comfort.

TOO FAST

CORRECT SPEED (5-10 SECONDS)

If you inject multiple compounds daily (e.g., BPC-157 + MOTS-c), use different injection sites for each compound. Do NOT mix peptides in the same syringe unless specifically directed by a protocol (TB-500 + GHK-Cu is one accepted combination). Each compound gets its own syringe and its own site.

| Site | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Upper outer thigh | Systemic compounds | Good fat pad; easy self-injection |
| Back of upper arm | Systemic compounds | May need partner for access |
| Love handle area | Systemic compounds | Ample subcutaneous tissue |

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Small welt or bump | Injected too fast or too shallow | Slow down; ensure needle is fully inserted |
| Bruising | Hit a capillary | Normal; rotate sites. Apply light pressure |
| Stinging/burning | Injection too fast, or alcohol not dry | Let alcohol dry 10 sec; inject over 5-10 sec |
| Liquid leaking out | Removed needle too quickly | Count to 5 after plunger fully depressed before withdrawing |
| Redness at site | Histamine response or repeat same site | Rotate sites strictly; if persistent, consult provider |
| Hard lump that persists | Lipodystrophy from poor rotation | Avoid that site for 2+ weeks; improve rotation |
