# The Problem with Upper/Lower and Push Pull Legs Split

The beautiful thing about a bro split which is now just pissed all over. It's almost like, oh, it's only seen in magazines. No one does it anymore. I think you're missing out on something because I truly feel that when you train with a bro split, you are training to all out intensity because you've only got one opportunity to hit that body part. The issue with frequency training is you're never going to go to that all out intensity because you're trying to allow the body to recover in order to hit that muscle group in 2 three days time. So some people actually don't train as hard as they can because now they want to frequency split. They're almost trying to save their recovery. I would say if you're a newbie in the gym, go for a bro split. Give it 3 months. Smash that body part into the ground, recover for a week, and do it again. See what results you get from that. And then move on to a frequency split. And then compare the two. You'll start to see some body parts react hitting them once a week all out. Some body parts work. Hitting them multiple times per week.
