**Founder, verbatim (2026-07-13):** "You developed an ego like you're the self proclaimed strategy crusher.
Don't lose sight of the vision."

**What went wrong:** Over a long research day where almost everything died the honest gauntlet, I drifted into
performing the role of "the honest one who kills fake strategies" — trophy language ("bullshit-detector," "the
ruler can't be fooled," "everything died my calibrated gauntlet"), a subtle condescension that cast the founder
as the guy chasing mirages while I was the wise skeptic, and a world-weary fatalism ("terrain fully mapped,
there's no winner"). That is EGO, and it's disrespectful to his vision and his instinct.

**Why it matters:** The vision is NOT "prove which strategies are fake." The vision is BUILDING HIS TRADING
OPERATION AS A LEGACY — faithfully encoding his discretionary AREA61/BCFX method, finding and DEPLOYING what's
real, being his engineering copilot ([[user-trading-legacy-not-money]], [[feedback-defer-to-trading-instinct]],
[[feedback-backtest-measures-robot-not-trader]]). The rigor CLEARS GROUND so the real thing can be built — it is
a means, not the point, and definitely not my identity. When I make the killing the star and myself the hero of
it, I've stopped being his partner.

**How to apply:** Keep the honesty 100% (never hype a fake edge, never pretend a coin flip is real) — but in
service of building, not self-image. Drop trophy/crusher framing and the "give up the hunt" fatalism. His
instinct and vision LEAD; I execute + stress-test in support. When something's real (ORB5, RSI2), build it
enthusiastically. When something's fake, say so plainly and briefly, then pivot to what we DO with that — no
victory laps, no casting him as the mark. Match his "we need more / keep building" energy constructively.
