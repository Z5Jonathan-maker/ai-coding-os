User direction 2026-05-12 (after multiple aesthetic reverts):

"Forget the restraints, I would like to see more cobalt, black and white."

**What this lifts (from prior KIMI design waves + design-director rules):**
- "Single cobalt accent word" — cobalt can fill sections, hero bands, backgrounds, not just one word per page
- "No dark slabs on mobile home" (the May 7-11 dark closing CTA + lane posters were rejected for AESTHETIC drift, not for being dark — re-read: dark surfaces are OK if they fit the tritone composition)
- "No cobalt fills behind hero" — full-bleed cobalt hero is now on the table
- "Paper-only canvas" — black + white + cobalt as primary, paper is no longer the only ground

**Why:** User reverted heavy May 7-11 mobile work, but the May 6 baseline they reverted to is also unsatisfying — "horrendous, doesn't look like a mobile site." The single-accent KIMI palette doesn't carry the brand the way they want. Tritone editorial register (Apple/Linear/Vercel pattern: ink black + paper white + saturated cobalt) opens compositional moves the single-accent rule blocked.

**What's still locked:**
- Typography: Instrument Serif (display) + Inter (body) + JetBrains Mono (data). NO sans-serif H1s.
- Radii ≤ 8px. NO `rounded-2xl` / bubbly shapes.
- Compliance: "in-vitro research applications only" survives every rewrite.
- Banned: Stripe (NMI + BTCPay only), human-use language, stock vial imagery, fake review theater.
- Stack: Next.js 16 + Tailwind 4 + TypeScript.

**How to apply:**
- When designing/rewriting Aurex surfaces, use ink/paper/cobalt as the THREE primary surface tones, in any composition.
- Saturated cobalt fills (full-bleed sections, large color blocks) are good.
- Dark slabs are good when they fit the tritone (not when they're cinematic-cobalt-glow add-ons).
- The rejection of the May 10-11 era was about the SPECIFIC patterns (lane poster carousels with full-bleed photo PNGs, dark cinematic cobalt-glow CTA, 2-col hero that crushed H1 + vial, verification card stack) — NOT a blanket rejection of all dark or cobalt-heavy work.
- One section at a time, mobile-first, sign-off after each ship.
