BEFORE YOU START
What You Need
Equipment Checklist
Gather everything before you begin. Working in a clean, well-lit area reduces contamination risk and

CRITICAL — WATER SELECTION
NEVER use tap water. Never use plain sterile water (it has no preservative — bacteria will grow within
hours). Bacteriostatic water ONLY. The benzyl alcohol preservative is what keeps your reconstituted
peptide safe for weeks.
Bacteriostatic Water (BAC Water)
Sterile water with 0.9% benzyl alcohol
preservative. This preservative prevents
bacterial growth after reconstitution, keeping
your peptide safe for 25-30 days.
30mL vials are standard. One vial lasts many
reconstitutions.
29-Gauge Insulin Syringes
Ultra-thin needle minimizes tissue damage and
pain. 1mL capacity with 100-unit markings. Use
a fresh syringe for every injection — never
reuse.
0.5mL syringes work for smaller doses.
Alcohol Swabs
70% isopropyl alcohol prep pads. Swab every
vial top before every needle insertion. Swab
injection site before every injection. Non-
negotiable hygiene step.
Buy in bulk — you'll use hundreds.
Lyophilized Peptide Vials
Your peptide arrives as a freeze-dried powder
(a small puck or cake at the bottom of the vial).
This is stable at room temp until reconstituted —
but refrigeration extends shelf life.
Check mg amount on label before reconstituting.

THE PROCESS
Step-by-Step
Reconstitution
Follow these steps exactly. Rushing or skipping steps destroys peptides and wastes money.
1 Clean Both Vial Tops with Alcohol Swab
Wipe the rubber stopper of both the BAC water vial and the peptide vial. Let air dry for 5 seconds. This
prevents contamination from manufacturing residue and handling.
2 Draw 1mL of BAC Water into Syringe
Insert needle into BAC water vial. Invert the vial. Slowly draw 1mL (100 units on an insulin syringe). Remove air
bubbles by tapping the syringe and pushing them out.
3 Insert Needle into Peptide Vial — Aim for the SIDE
Pierce the rubber stopper at a slight angle. Position the needle tip so it touches the inside wall of the glass
vial, NOT pointing directly at the powder.
4 Slowly Push BAC Water In — Drip Down the Side
Push the plunger extremely slowly. The water should trickle down the inside wall of the vial onto the powder.
DO NOT squirt directly onto the powder. Direct force breaks peptide bonds.
5 Remove Syringe — DO NOT Shake
Withdraw the needle carefully. Set the vial down gently. Resist every urge to shake it. Shaking denatures the
peptide chains and renders them useless.
6 Gently Swirl Between Your Palms
Roll the vial between your palms with light pressure. Think: "treat it like a newborn." Gentle rotation helps the
powder dissolve without damaging the fragile peptide structure.
7 Allow to Dissolve Fully (5-10 Minutes)
Set the vial upright in the refrigerator. Most peptides dissolve within 5-10 minutes. Some may take longer. The
solution should be completely clear — no floating particles or cloudiness.
8 Refrigerate Immediately
Once dissolved, the vial stays in the fridge at 36-46°F. Every minute at room temperature reduces potency.
Mark the vial with the reconstitution date. Use within 25-30 days.
HANDLE WITH CARE
Peptides are fragments — they are fragile. They are short chains of amino acids held together by delicate
bonds. Aggressive handling (shaking, squirting, heat exposure) physically destroys these bonds. A
degraded peptide is a wasted peptide.

The Formula
Peptide Amount (mcg) ÷ BAC Water (mL) = Concentration (mcg/mL)
Then: Desired Dose (mcg) ÷ Concentration (mcg/mL) = Volume to Inject (mL)

STORAGE & SAFETY
Keep Your Peptides
Safe & Potent
Proper storage is the difference between a peptide that works and expensive water.
Storage Rules
STATE STORAGE METHOD DURATION
Pre-reconstitution (powder) Freezer or refrigerator Months to years
Post-reconstitution (liquid) Refrigerator only (36-46°F) 25-30 days max
Freeze after reconstitution? NEVER Destroys peptide structure
Room temperature (post-recon)? NEVER Rapid degradation + bacterial growth
5 Common Mistakes That Destroy Your Peptides

1 Shaking the Vial
Shaking creates micro-bubbles and shear forces that break peptide bonds. Always swirl gently. Never
shake, tap, or flick.
2 Using Regular Water or Sterile Water
Tap water introduces bacteria, minerals, and chlorine. Sterile water (without benzyl alcohol) has no
preservative — bacteria colonize within hours. BAC water only.
3 Leaving at Room Temperature
Reconstituted peptides degrade rapidly above 46°F. Every hour at room temp reduces potency. Get it
back in the fridge immediately after drawing your dose.
4 Reusing Needles
Reused needles are dull (causing tissue damage), contaminated (introducing bacteria into the vial),
and dangerous. One syringe per injection. No exceptions.
5 Not Rotating Injection Sites
Injecting the same spot repeatedly causes lipodystrophy (fat tissue damage), scarring, and reduced
absorption. Rotate: abdomen (left/right), thighs, upper arms.
